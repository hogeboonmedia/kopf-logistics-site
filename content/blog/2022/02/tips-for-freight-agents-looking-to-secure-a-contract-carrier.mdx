<h2><b>Tips for Freight Agents Looking to Secure a Contract Carrier</b></h2>
<p><b>The value of local freight trucking in the United States is around </b><a href="https://www.ibisworld.com/industry-statistics/market-size/local-freight-trucking-united-states/"><b>$67.3 billion</b></a><b>. This rate is far from where it was in 2019 to early 2020, when Covid-19 travel restrictions hurt the industry. In 2021, the demand to move goods by road realized a significant increase.<span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>The competition for shipping goods is growing more challenging every day and sourcing a quality Contract Carrier can be a struggle.<span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>Here are a few tips for Freight Agents that will assist you in finding quality Contract Carriers.</b></p>
<h4><b>Assess The Competition</b></h4>
<p><b>Competition is standard and a driving factor of capitalism. Remember, any product transported by freight has competitors. When considering a Contract Carrier, research their competition. <span class="Apple-converted-space"> </span></b></p>
<h4><b>Relationship Is Key</b></h4>
<p><b>Being a dependable and responsible Freight Agent is one of the best ways to recruit Carriers. This means maintaining business ethics.<span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>Carriers will most likely do their best work and even refer others to you if you are responsible.<span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>Pay them on time, communicate as a professional, and fulfill your commitments.</b></p>
<p><b>Also, know the Federal Motor Carrier Safety Administration&#8217;s </b><a href="https://www.fmcsa.dot.gov/regulations/hours-service/summary-hours-service-regulations"><b>Hours of Service Regulations</b></a><b>. These regulations include the service hours limitations for property and passenger-carrying Drivers alike.<span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>Be careful not to violate service hours.<span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>This can compromise the cargo as well as the Driver’s safety.<span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>Maltreating your Carriers will damage your reputation. As a responsible Freight Agent, make sure to treat Carriers well.<span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>Maintaining a good relationship will benefit you in the long run.</b></p>
<h4><b>Cold Calling Works</b></h4>
<p><b>Cold calling has been around as long as the telephone.<span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>Freight Agents can greatly benefit from making unsolicited calls to potential new Carriers, but cold calling can be challenging to execute correctly. Before calling, gain relevant information about the prospect, master your sales pitch and remember to make a human connection. <span class="Apple-converted-space"> </span></b></p>
<h4><b>Social Media Advertisement</b></h4>
<p><b>Make use of social media. Almost everyone has access to this platform. Social media can boost your posts for broader reach.<span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>To secure interested Carriers, make your offer appealing. Highlight location and discuss the benefit to them.</b></p>
