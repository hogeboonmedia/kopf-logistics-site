<h2>When Do Truckers Have to Undergo a DOT Drug Screen?</h2>
<p>It&#8217;s estimated there are roughly <a href="https://www.census.gov/library/stories/2019/06/america-keeps-on-trucking.html%23:~:text=More%2520than%25203.5%2520million%2520people,occupations%2520in%2520the%2520United%2520States.">3.5 million truck drivers</a> in the United States. With so many people working as CDL Drivers, it&#8217;s no wonder employers want to take this important role as seriously as possible.<span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>One of the main ways this is done is through DOT drug testing. The tests check to make sure alcohol levels are below 0.02% and that drugs like marijuana, cocaine, opiates, or amphetamines aren&#8217;t in a Driver&#8217;s system.<span class="Apple-converted-space"> </span></p>
<p>So when can a Truck Driver expect to take a test? How does a DOT drug screen work? Here&#8217;s a quick overview of everything you need to know.<span class="Apple-converted-space"> </span></p>
<h4><b>DOT Drug Screen Before Employment</b></h4>
<p>Like many other types of jobs, <a href="https://kopflogisticsgroup.com/drivers/">a driver</a> must take a drug and alcohol test as part of the hiring process before operating a commercial motor vehicle.<span class="Apple-converted-space"> </span></p>
<p>A CDL Driver must take a drug test before officially starting the job. This is part of the screening phase and is an important indicator as to whether a potential Driver will be responsible and reliable.<span class="Apple-converted-space"> </span></p>
<h4><b>Random Testing Throughout the Year</b></h4>
<p>After a Driver begins working, they will be subject to <a href="https://www.fmcsa.dot.gov/regulations/title49/section/382.305">random drug and alcohol testing</a> throughout their time working for a company. According to DOT drug testing requirements, random testing is done throughout the year, but some companies choose to do more frequent testing to be extra sure their Drivers are being safe.<span class="Apple-converted-space"> </span></p>
<p>A random selection pool decides when CDL Drivers take a drug test. Once selected, a Driver has to take the test as soon as possible.<span class="Apple-converted-space"> </span></p>
<h4><b>After an Accident</b></h4>
<p>If a Driver is involved in an accident while on the job, it&#8217;s possible they&#8217;ll need to submit to a DOT drug screening depending on the accident&#8217;s circumstances.<span class="Apple-converted-space"> </span></p>
<p>For example, accidents that result in a death or that require medical attention away from where the accident happened would require drug testing. A Driver would need to take an alcohol test within 8 hours after the accident or a drug test within 32 hours.<span class="Apple-converted-space"> </span></p>
<h4><b>Testing Due to Reasonable Suspicion</b></h4>
<p>Sometimes a Driver may be asked to submit to drug screening because their employer is concerned they might be using or abusing drugs or alcohol that affect their work.</p>
<p>This doesn&#8217;t mean anyone can make a Driver take a drug test. In most cases, there&#8217;s an employee for the company who&#8217;s trained to spot the symptoms that might be a cause for concern.<span class="Apple-converted-space"> </span></p>
<h4><b>Return-to-Duty Testing</b></h4>
<p>If a Driver fails a drug or alcohol test, there&#8217;s a process they must go through to start driving again.<span class="Apple-converted-space"> </span></p>
<p>This process involves talking with a Substance Abuse Professional and finishing a required program. They also have to successfully pass another drug test before getting back on the road.</p>
<p>Once a Driver completes the program and passes the drug or alcohol test, they&#8217;ll also need to take at least six follow-up tests over the following year.<span class="Apple-converted-space"> </span></p>
<h4><b>Drive Carefully and Confidently<span class="Apple-converted-space"> </span></b></h4>
<p>The main purpose of a DOT drug screen is to make certain the roads are safe for everyone using them, including CDL Drivers.<span class="Apple-converted-space"> </span></p>
