<h2><b>4 Reasons To Become a Truck Owner-Operator</b></h2>
<p>So many of the things we enjoy and perhaps take for granted are made possible thanks to the hard work and skill of Truck Drivers.<span class="Apple-converted-space"> </span></p>
<p>In the United States, the trucking industry has a <a href="https://markets.businessinsider.com/news/stocks/trucking-industry-facts-us-truckers-2019-5">$700 billion</a> value. This means both consumers and professionals in the industry benefit from the huge demand.<span class="Apple-converted-space"> </span></p>
<p>If you&#8217;d like to make truck driving a career, it pays to consider becoming an Owner-Operator. Keep reading to learn more about the advantages that come with the Owner-Operator career track.<span class="Apple-converted-space"> </span></p>
<h4><b>1. Becoming a Truck Owner-Operator Lets You Be Your Own Boss</b></h4>
<p>When you decide to <a href="https://kopflogisticsgroup.com/2021/04/truck-driver-facts/">become a truck driver</a>, you&#8217;ll be glad to know you can do it in a way that allows you to take control of your destiny.<span class="Apple-converted-space"> </span></p>
<p>Owner-Operator trucking puts you in the driver&#8217;s seat, allowing you to control which jobs you take and which you deny. You&#8217;ll decide when you work and how much time you take off, and only answer to yourself and your business contacts each day.<span class="Apple-converted-space"> </span></p>
<p>If you are organized, a self-starter, and take care of business, you&#8217;ll love taking on the challenge.<span class="Apple-converted-space"> </span></p>
<h4><b>2. You Improve Your Earning Potential</b></h4>
<p>Today, <a href="https://www.truckinfo.net/research/trucking-statistics">one out of every nine</a> drivers works independently. Among these, several work as Owner-Operators.<span class="Apple-converted-space"> </span></p>
<p>Many people go the Owner-Operator route so they can improve their earnings. There&#8217;s no ceiling on how much pay you can take home when you&#8217;re working independently.<span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>This helps you set aside more money for retirement and will help you enter an entirely new tax bracket.<span class="Apple-converted-space"> </span></p>
<h4><b>3. These Jobs Are Highly Available and In-Demand</b></h4>
<p>Owner-Operators are incredibly in-demand.<span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>In 2020, there were close to <a href="https://www.bls.gov/ooh/transportation-and-material-moving/heavy-and-tractor-trailer-truck-drivers.htm">2 million</a> truck driving jobs, and the industry was growing at a 6% rate.<span class="Apple-converted-space"> </span></p>
<p>This level of security gives you peace of mind since job security equals life security. There are plenty of lateral or greater moves you can make if you&#8217;re ever out of work.<span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>As long as you remain available and keep your driving record clean, you shouldn&#8217;t have a problem landing jobs.<span class="Apple-converted-space"> </span></p>
<h4><b>4. The Travel and Job Perks Are Excellent</b></h4>
<p>While it&#8217;s not a lifestyle for everyone, people who love it wouldn&#8217;t have it any other way.<span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>Becoming an Owner-Operator increases your travel opportunities and you can partner with several companies for the work you take on.<span class="Apple-converted-space"> </span></p>
<p>Owner-Operators are also able to continue their education for more opportunities. There are several certifications you can receive to expand your opportunities.<span class="Apple-converted-space"> </span></p>
