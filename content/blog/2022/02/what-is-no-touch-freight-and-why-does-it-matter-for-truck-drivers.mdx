<h2>What Is No-Touch Freight and Why Does It Matter for Truck Drivers?</h2>
<p>No-touch freight is exactly what you think it is. You drive the truck but you don&#8217;t touch the freight you&#8217;re hauling. You&#8217;ll notice the term &#8220;no-touch freight&#8221; in various job vacancies, so it&#8217;s good to know exactly what it means.<span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>Why does it matter to Truck Drivers? Does no-touch freight really give Drivers the opportunity to relax while the freight is being cared for by someone else?<span class="Apple-converted-space"> </span></p>
<p>Well, in this short guide we&#8217;ll try to answer these questions. So let&#8217;s look into this matter a little further.</p>
<h4><b>No-Touch Freight Driving Jobs</b></h4>
<p>For many Truckers out there, the manual work of shifting freight can be back-breaking. No-touch freight is a great option for anyone that loves to focus on the driving aspect of trucking only.<span class="Apple-converted-space"> </span></p>
<p>No-touch freight means the shipper’s or consignee’s employees or warehouse workers load or unload a trailer/container parked at a loading dock while the driver rests in the truck cab. <span class="Apple-converted-space"> </span></p>
<h4><b>Hauling Specific Loads</b></h4>
<p>There are specific loads that need highly qualified individuals to care for them. Some of these loads include <a href="https://www.faa.gov/hazmat/what_is_hazmat/">dangerous goods</a>, toxic chemicals, or gases for instance.<span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>Drivers and motor carriers transporting hazardous materials share a responsibility to know their obligations as required by the Federal Hazardous Materials Regulations, most specifically how the requirements apply to their duties. <span class="Apple-converted-space"> </span></p>
<h4><b>You Get Some Chill Time</b></h4>
<p>One great thing about being a no-touch freight Driver is when you pull in to drop off a load and pick up another load you&#8217;ll have time to relax.<span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>This is a perfect time to rest your eyes, grab a coffee, or call home.<span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>It&#8217;s also a good time to stretch your legs and enjoy the scenery.<span class="Apple-converted-space"> </span></p>
<h4><b>There&#8217;s less Liability for You</b></h4>
<p>Finally, opting for no-touch trucking jobs means you have less overall liability. This may be <a href="https://www.ttnews.com/articles/examining-sources-driver-stress">less stressful</a> for many Truck Drivers who just want to drive.<span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>Operating a truck on the road is enough liability for any given Driver.<span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>Handling the freight on a loading dock adds a whole new level of liability to that.<span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>With elevated platforms, heavy cargo, and machinery like forklifts and dock ramps, things can go wrong in a loading bay.<span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>With no-touch freight, loading zones have their own team that knows the proper procedures which takes the pressure off you. <span class="Apple-converted-space"> </span></p>
<h4><b>The No-Touch Freight Option</b></h4>
<p>No-touch freight driving is well worth consideration. If your focus is driving only, you should consider this type of work.</p>
