<p>Did you know the logistics industry is worth <a href="https://www.alliedmarketresearch.com/logistics-market">over eight trillion dollars</a> to the US economy each year? Logistics is the backbone of business, providing a strong base for all kinds of commerce.<span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>But many people forget about this vital component of business. They might not be aware of how poorly managed logistics processes can cause issues in the supply chain.<span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>Want to know more about the ways poorly managed logistics can impact your business? Read on! We&#8217;ve put together this list to help you better understand the issue.<span class="Apple-converted-space"> </span></p>
<h4>1. Your Products Could be Damaged</h4>
<p>Logistics isn&#8217;t just about moving goods from one point to another. It&#8217;s also about packaging and handling.<span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>A company that does not focus on quality might not give your products proper attention. <span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>When companies look to cut corners, they&#8217;re unlikely to be as careful as they should be. They might damage your goods in transit, costing you money and damaging your reputation.</p>
<h4>2. Low Efficiency</h4>
<p>Great businesses are made up of constantly moving parts. Products or services should move along a kind of imaginary production line (or sometimes a real one) to keep things flowing and maintain peak efficiency.<span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>Poor logistics processes will damage your efficiency and cause the rest of your operations to lag behind.<span class="Apple-converted-space">   </span></p>
<h4>3. Lose Customers</h4>
<p>When your supply chain breaks down, it doesn’t just cause you to lose sales over the course of a day. It can have long-term effects on your business, causing you to <a href="https://www.entrepreneur.com/article/313463">lose customers</a>.<span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>Let&#8217;s imagine you&#8217;re running a bakery. You&#8217;ve got shipments ready to roll out, but your shipping services let you down.<span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>The stores that were going to sell your fresh products won’t be able to stock their shelves that day. They might choose to find a different supplier going forward. It doesn&#8217;t matter that it wasn&#8217;t your fault, business is business and a slip-up like this can be costly.<span class="Apple-converted-space"> </span></p>
<h4>4. The Domino Effect</h4>
<p>Some say bad things come in threes. Whether that&#8217;s true or not, when one thing goes wrong in business, more mishaps are generally just around the corner!<span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>Let&#8217;s go back to the bakery example. Without a good logistics system in place, customers are lost. But that&#8217;s not the end of their worries. Their cases are full of goodies they might not be able to quickly sell.<span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>This is just an example, but it demonstrates how when one thing goes wrong, it can have a knock-on effect that could cost you greatly.<span class="Apple-converted-space"> </span></p>
