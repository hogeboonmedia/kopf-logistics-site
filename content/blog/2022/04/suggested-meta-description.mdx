<p>Did you know the U.S. freight packing and <a href="https://www.ibisworld.com/united-states/market-research-reports/freight-packing-logistics-services-industry/">logistics industry</a> has a market size of $3 billion? There are over 8,000 businesses offering jobs in logistics.<span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>Have you considered different careers in logistics but don&#8217;t know where to start?<span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>Do you know the logistics skills that each job type requires?<span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>The following guide will explore 5 exciting logistics careers and detail what they involve. Read on and discover which logistics position is right for you.<span class="Apple-converted-space"> </span></p>
<h4><b>1. Freight Agent</b></h4>
<p>Freight agents manage and track shipments through road, rail, air, and maritime transportation. They make sure shipments are delivered and received on time on behalf of the client.<span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>This position requires advanced organizational skills. The <a href="https://kopflogisticsgroup.com/2022/02/tips-for-freight-agents-looking-to-secure-a-contract-carrier/">freight agent</a> implements arrangement methods and coordinates route planning. They also take client orders, process paperwork and negotiate payments.</p>
<h4><b>2. Supply Chain Manager</b></h4>
<p>The <a href="https://www.investopedia.com/terms/s/scm.asp">supply chain manager</a> is a crucial logistics position within a company. It requires excellent managerial and communication skills. Supply chain management involves overseeing the equipment and raw materials of a company.<span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>Tasks include buying, warehousing, inventory, and production of different distribution products. Supply chain managers help streamline the process to lower costs and boost productivity.</p>
<h4><b>3. Warehouse Logistics Manager</b></h4>
<p>A warehouse logistics manager helps control and maintain the stock of a business.<span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>They manage the storage of company goods and warehouse teams. Managers also process orders and arrange the delivery of materials.<span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>Warehouse logistics management ensures the quality, quantity, and safety of stocks. They&#8217;re also responsible for training warehouse staff and maintaining health and safety standards.<span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>This position protects companies from unexpected and costly events such as low inventory and shipment delays, which often causes production to stop completely.<span class="Apple-converted-space">   </span></p>
<h4><b>4. Transportation Analyst</b></h4>
<p>Transportation analysts improve the transportation and supply strategy of goods. They use software to plan the movement of company supplies. The goal is to save money and time throughout a product&#8217;s lifecycle.<span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>Transportation analysts help build relationships with suppliers and clients. This position requires computer knowledge, communication skills, and organizational skills.<span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>Data analysis, project management, and cost reduction are key in the work of a transportation analyst. <span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>Analysts generate constant improvements to different technologies and processes, improving the transportation experience.</p>
<h4><b>5. Logistics Engineer</b></h4>
<p>Logistics engineers often have a background in science and mathematics. They help create and review different processes for the company. Their goal is to optimize the distribution of goods and services.<span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>Engineers use method analysis, capacity enhancement, and routing to enhance shipments throughout a product&#8217;s supply chain and help create efficient distribution to produce higher profits for the company. Common methods include inventory planning, order processing, and shipment strategies.</p>
