<h2><span style="font-weight: 400;">What is Freight Contract Bidding?</span></h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Before we dive into the practical part of the guide, let’s define what contract bidding is. Freight contract bidding is</span><b> how Shippers request and review offers from Freight Brokers and Agents to arrange transportation services under agreed terms.</b></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">This is a highly competitive process with the goal of winning freight contracts that guarantee business. There are three main types of contracts:</span></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><b>Spot contracts:</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> short-term agreements used for one-time or immediate shipments, with pricing often based on current market rates. </span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><b>Lane-based contracts:</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> focus on specific routes or lanes and typically offer consistent volume over a medium length term. </span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><b>Annual contracts</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> (also known as long-term or dedicated contracts)</span><b>:</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> these contracts lock in rates and service agreements for a full year or more, providing greater predictability for both the Shipper and the Agent.</span></li>
</ul>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Each contract type requires a tailored bidding strategy to remain competitive and profitable.</span><span style="font-weight: 400;"><br />
</span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h2><span style="font-weight: 400;">Why Effective Bidding Matters</span></h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">In tight or highly competitive markets, like we are seeing now, a well-executed bid can provide an edge over other Agents by offering the right balance of price, service, and reliability.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Strong bidding strategies also have a direct impact on </span><a href="https://kopflogisticsgroup.com/2023/01/increase-profit-margins/"><b>profit margins</b></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">. It is important to avoid underpricing and accurately model costs. Agents want to make sure every contract is realistic and profitable. </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Additionally, effective bidding supports </span><a href="https://kopflogisticsgroup.com/2022/10/why-customer-retention-is-important-for-freight-agents/"><b>client retention</b></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> by demonstrating professionalism, consistency, and a clear understanding of a Shipper’s needs. This also helps build trust and increase the likelihood of long-term partnerships. </span></p>
<p><b>Bottom line: Mastering the freight bidding process is not just about winning loads. It is about growing a sustainable and competitive Freight Agency.</b></p>

<h2><span style="font-weight: 400;">How to Bid on Freight Contracts</span></h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Bidding on freight contracts is a strategic multi-step process. Each step in this process plays a role in positioning yourself as a reliable, cost-effective partner. Here’s how it typically works.</span><b></b></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h3><span style="font-weight: 400;">Step 1: Research Opportunities</span></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">The first step is to research high-value RFPs (Request for Proposal) and RFQs (Request for Quote). These can come through load boards, Shipper networks, and industry contacts. </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Carefully examine the lanes, shipment volumes, service expectations, and contract duration. Be sure to understand the client’s goals and any special requirements. Also assess lane demand and carrier capacity. </span></p>
<h3><span style="font-weight: 400;">Step 2: Build an Accurate Cost Model</span></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">After researching opportunities, build an accurate cost model for each lane or service category to ensure your bid is both competitive and sustainable.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Use historical rate data, market trends, and your carrier network’s capabilities to assess each lane. This helps determine which routes are profitable and align with your operations.  </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">There are also many other factors to consider including: margin targets, risk buffers, linehaul rates, fuel surcharges, and accessorial fees. </span></p>
<h3><span style="font-weight: 400;">Step 3: Develop a Competitive Pricing Strategy</span><span style="font-weight: 400;"><br />
</span></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Using your cost model, develop a competitive pricing strategy that balances aggressive rates with sustainable profit margins. Consider offering discounts tied to volume commitments to enhance value while maintaining long-term profitability.</span></p>
<h3><span style="font-weight: 400;">Step 4: Ensure Regulatory and Carrier Compliance</span></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">This step is often overlooked, but it can significantly reduce the risk of bid rejection and streamline the rest of the process. You can ensure regulatory and carrier compliance by pre-qualifying carriers in advance. Confirm their insurance, licensing, and surety bonds. If you already have a reliable network of carriers, you’ll be at an advantage and this step will be much simpler.</span></p>
<h3><span style="font-weight: 400;">Step 5: Submit a Persuasive Proposal Document</span><b><br />
</b></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">After the first four steps are complete, you are now ready to craft and your proposal before the dates specified by the Shipper. Present your rates along with supporting details like your service capabilities, technology tools, carrier vetting process, and past performance metrics. Highlight what makes you different, just as you would in a </span><a href="https://kopflogisticsgroup.com/2020/06/best-cold-call-script-for-freight-agents/"><b>cold call.</b></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> You can also include a service guarantee and escalation clause. </span></p>
<h3><span style="font-weight: 400;">Step 6: Negotiate if Needed and Finalize Terms</span><b><br />
</b></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">After submitting your proposal, be prepared for follow-up discussions or counteroffers. If you have strong negotiation skills you can help finalize a win-win agreement without compromising your margins (</span><a href="https://kopflogisticsgroup.com/2020/05/how-to-negotiate-freight-rates-top-tips-for-success-in-logistics/"><span style="font-weight: 400;">use these 5 secret negotiation strategies for maximum profit</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">).</span><span style="font-weight: 400;"><br />
</span></p>
<h3><span style="font-weight: 400;">Step 7: Track and Learn</span><b><br />
</b></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Whether you win the bid or not, view each submission as a learning experience. Track key details such as pricing, lanes, service requirements, win/loss outcomes, and feedback from Shippers. Analyze this information to refine your future bids and sharpen your overall strategy. </span></p>

<h2><span style="font-weight: 400;">Common Pitfalls and How to Avoid Them</span></h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">There are two common mistakes in contract bidding and both of these can reduce profit margins and damage client trust.</span></p>
<h3><span style="font-weight: 400;">Underestimating Accessorial Fees</span></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Accessorial fees are the extra charges for services beyond standard pickup and delivery like detention, layovers, liftgate use, or residential delivery. If overlooked or underquoted, these fees can turn a seemingly profitable contract into a financial burden.</span><span style="font-weight: 400;"><br />
</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Avoid this pitfall by building a comprehensive accessorial fee structure into your cost model. Use historical data and carrier input to anticipate common add-ons for each lane, and clearly outline them in your proposal to avoid disputes later.</span></p>
<h3><span style="font-weight: 400;">Failing to Update Cost Models for Market Shifts</span></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Freight markets are dynamic. They are influenced by fuel prices, seasonality, capacity constraints, and regulatory changes. Using outdated rate data can result in underbidding (and losing money) or overbidding (and losing contracts).</span><span style="font-weight: 400;"><br />
</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">One way to avoid this pitfall is to regularly update your cost models using real-time data from your TMS, market indexes (like DAT or FreightWaves), and carrier feedback. Automate this process where possible to ensure every bid reflects current conditions.</span></p>
