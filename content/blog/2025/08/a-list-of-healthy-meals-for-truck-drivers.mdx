<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">If you are a Truck Driver, we have a question for you. Do you keep your truck fueled and well-maintained? Of course you do! If your truck runs out of gas or breaks down, you can’t do your job. </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">But, what about yourself? Are you keeping your body fueled and prioritizing </span><i><span style="font-weight: 400;">your </span></i><span style="font-weight: 400;">health and well-being? That’s not so easy, right? </span></p>
<p><b>At Kopf we understand it can be difficult to prioritize </b><a href="https://kopflogisticsgroup.com/2020/12/trucker-healthy-eating/"><b>healthy eating</b></a><b> and </b><a href="https://kopflogisticsgroup.com/how-drivers-can-stay-fit-on-the-road-tips-workout-ideas/"><b>stay fit with an on-the-road lifestyle.</b></a><b> But if you want to make a change, we want to help because YOU are our most important asset! Without YOU, the entire transportation industry comes to a grinding halt. </b></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;"><br />
</span><span style="font-weight: 400;">So let’s take a look at why healthy eating matters, key components of a healthy diet, our list of realistic and nutritious on-the-road healthy meal ideas (including healthy truck stop options), as well as tips for maintaining a healthy diet. Whether you&#8217;re a long-haul driver or regional operator, these healthy trucker meals will keep your energy high and keep you feeling your best.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Ready? Let’s go!</span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h2><span style="font-weight: 400;">Why Healthy Eating Matters</span><span style="font-weight: 400;"><br />
</span></h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">The time to start paying attention to what you are eating isn’t when you’re having health challenges. It is now. </span></p>
<h3><span style="font-weight: 400;">Your Health</span></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Driving a truck is a sedentary job. After all, you are sitting behind the wheel most of the day. But this statistic is eye-opening: </span><b>Truckers are TWICE as likely as other workers to be obese, putting you at a higher risk of developing diabetes, high blood pressure, and heart disease</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> (</span><a href="https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/abs/10.1002/ajim.22293"><span style="font-weight: 400;">see this study</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">).</span></p>
<h3><span style="font-weight: 400;">Your Safety</span></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">There is a clear link between diet, energy, and alertness. Think of food as fuel. Higher quality fuel (meaning nutrient dense food) keeps your engine running at a higher level (increased energy). </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">In </span><a href="https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/34404677/"><span style="font-weight: 400;">this study of 389 Truck Drivers</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">, researchers concluded: </span></p>
<blockquote><p><b> “The driving behaviors of truck drivers are directly correlated with their dietary patterns. </b><span style="font-weight: 400;">Drivers who preferred vegetables and staple foods had more positive driving behavior, while the animal-derived food and snack patterns were related to dangerous driving behavior. The experience of fatigue could explain the underlying mechanism between these factors.”</span></p></blockquote>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">And while poor sleep and lack of exercise are also factors in fatigue, poor nutrition is also a source. </span></p>
<p><b>Fatigue reduces alertness, reaction time, and coordination for Drivers on the road. </b></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">The </span><a href="https://www.ntsb.gov/safety/safety-studies/Pages/SS9502.aspx"><span style="font-weight: 400;">National Transportation Safety Board</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> found in studying data from heavy truck accidents “31% incidence of fatigue in fatal-to-the-driver accidents.”</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Bottom line: poor nutrition is detrimental to your health and safety on the road, which leads to our next question.</span><span style="font-weight: 400;"><br />
</span><span style="font-weight: 400;"><br />
</span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h2><span style="font-weight: 400;">What is a Healthy Truck Driver Diet?</span></h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">If nutrition is so important, what kinds of food should Truck Drivers be eating? There are a few key components to prioritize and foods to limit.</span></p>
<h3><span style="font-weight: 400;">Prioritize: </span></h3>
<ul>
<li><b>Lean proteins:</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> egg, cottage cheese, Greek yogurt, beef, chicken breast, turkey, pork loin, fish, nuts, quinoa, legumes</span></li>
<li><b>Fiber:</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> fruits, vegetables, grains, legumes, nuts, seeds</span></li>
<li><b>Complex carbs: </b><span style="font-weight: 400;">whole grains (brown rice, oats, quinoa, barley, whole wheat), legumes like lentils and chickpeas, starchy vegetables like potatoes and peas, fruits like berries</span></li>
<li><b>Healthy fats: </b><span style="font-weight: 400;">avocados, olives, coconut, fatty fish, nuts, eggs, full fat yogurt</span></li>
<li><b>Hydration: </b><span style="font-weight: 400;">water, watery fruits like watermelon and cantaloupe, lettuce, soups and broths</span></li>
<li style="list-style-type: none;"></li>
</ul>
<h3><span style="font-weight: 400;">Limit: </span></h3>
<ul>
<li aria-level="1"><b>Processed foods: </b><span style="font-weight: 400;">sugary cereals and snack bars, white bread, pastries</span></li>
<li aria-level="1"><b>Sugary drinks: </b><span style="font-weight: 400;">soda, energy drinks, sweetened beverages</span></li>
<li aria-level="1"><b>High fat snacks: </b><span style="font-weight: 400;">fried food, chips </span></li>
</ul>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">We are not recommending counting calories or eating bland foods. Rather, prioritize a variety of foods that give you energy and nourish your body. </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">The standard American diet (known as “SAD” for a reason) is often full of beige, tan, and brown. If you want to have a more balanced daily intake on the road, take a look at how many colors you are eating. The more bright colors the better (hint: bright colors are found in plants). </span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h2><span style="font-weight: 400;">Best Healthy Meals for Truck Drivers</span></h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Keeping the key components of a healthy diet in mind (lean proteins, fruits, vegetables, healthy fats, water), there are infinite meal options for Truck Drivers. Whether you are prepping meals at home, eating at a truck stop or cooking in your cab, use this list as inspiration. Ultimately, the best healthy meals are ones you will eat!</span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h3><span style="font-weight: 400;">1. Prepped Meals You Can Pack from Home</span></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">For short hauls, the best way to eat healthy is to come prepared! Here are some healthy meal options: Grilled chicken with brown rice and veggies, turkey wraps with hummus and spinach, or quinoa salad with black beans, corn, and avocado.</span><span style="font-weight: 400;"><br />
</span></p>
<h3><span style="font-weight: 400;">2. Healthy Truck Stop Options</span></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">We do like a good truck stop (</span><a href="https://kopflogisticsgroup.com/2021/05/top-3-truck-stops-for-drivers-across-the-country/"><span style="font-weight: 400;">especially these three</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">). Fuel your body well by keeping these things in mind: </span></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Choose grilled options over fried at fast food chains</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Look for salad bars, yogurt, boiled eggs, and fruit cups</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Look for whole grain over white bread</span><span style="font-weight: 400;"><br />
</span></li>
</ul>
<h3><span style="font-weight: 400;">3. High-Protein Snacks for Long Hauls</span></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Protein, healthy fats, and fiber helps to keep your body full longer. There is some debate over how much protein a person needs in a day. Pay attention to what your body is telling you. And remember, water is important too!</span></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Low-sodium jerky </span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Almonds</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Trail mix </span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Protein bars (be careful of added sugars)</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Greek yogurt</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Cottage cheese</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Hard-boiled eggs</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Hummus and vegetables</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Peanut butter and apples</span></li>
</ul>

<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Sometimes it is just easier and more affordable to drink a meal. Look for at least 20 grams of protein, low sugar, and fiber. </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Some popular brands Truckers trust: </span></p>
<ul>

<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">You can also make your own high protein shakes by adding legumes or nut butters to a blender with fruit and milk.  Simply do a quick search for “protein shake recipes” and try one until you find one you like. </span></p>
<h3><span style="font-weight: 400;">5. Cooking in the Truck Cab</span></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">For long hauls, you can also cook your own meals in your cab if you are prepared with cooking equipment (see “Tools and Gadgets” below). You could make chili with beans, oatmeal with nuts and dried fruit, chicken and sweet potato hash. It does take some planning and practice to get into a rhythm of cooking in the cab. </span><a href="https://www.tiktok.com/@real_truckerliam"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Trucker Liam</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> has a TikTok channel full of real food he cooks on the road. It is helpful to learn from other Truck Drivers!</span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h2><span style="font-weight: 400;">Hydration on the Highway</span></h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">It is important we pause to note that hydration is important too! Did you know dehydration can mimic fatigue?  Dehydration causes tiredness, poor judgement, and reduced reaction times. </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">The best way to stay hydrated is by drinking water. Energy drinks and soda are often loaded with sugar which is not ideal for your overall health. If you find it hard to drink plain water, create infusions by adding in fresh fruits (lemon, berries, cucumber) to give your water a mild flavor. You can also eat watery foods like watermelon, cantaloupe, and lettuce as well as soups and broths. </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Our best tip: always carry a water bottle! Fill bottles with as much water as you should drink for the day (at least 64 ounces) and as you stop for each meal, make sure those bottles are getting empty. </span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h2><span style="font-weight: 400;">How to Stick to Healthy Eating on the Road</span></h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">While all the meal ideas might sound great, putting them into practice is what matters most. Truckers can stick to healthy eating on the road by meal planning. Simply jot down the days of the week and what you plan to eat and make a grocery list of what you’ll need. Then go shopping to match that plan. Once you have found a list of several meals you like, make a list and use these as a simple weekly food rotation so you don’t have to reinvent the wheel each week. </span></p>
<h3><span style="font-weight: 400;">Apps </span></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Download any of these apps to your phone to make healthy eating easier on the road. </span></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><b>Rolling Strong:</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> This app is specifically designed for truckers. It includes wellness coaching, workout routines, and the ability to log food, water, and sleep habits. It also helps locate truck-friendly clinics and offers wellness seminars. </span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><b>BudgetBytes: </b><span style="font-weight: 400;">If you’re looking for budget-friendly recipes with cost breakdowns and calorie counts, this app is helpful. It can make it easier to eat healthy on a budget.</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><b>Iron Trucker: </b><span style="font-weight: 400;">A great app for Drivers who want to do exercises using the trailer. It also includes a food journal to help Drivers create a sustainable health and wellness routine. </span></li>
</ul>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">There are even apps for hydration reminders, grocery lists, calorie tracking, and more. Take advantage of any of these resources. </span></p>

<h2><span style="font-weight: 400;">Common Pitfalls to Avoid</span></h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">As with anything, if you fail to plan, you are planning to fail. So if eating healthy is important to you, take time to plan your meals and snacks in advance. If your cab is filled with healthy options and you’re keeping water next to you, you’re more likely to reach for the foods that will fuel your body well. If you skip meals, it is easier to binge later on sugary snacks, processed foods, and oversized portions. </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Don’t fall for “low fat” labels. Those foods are often loaded with sugar. Healthy fats are an essential part of a healthy diet. </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Lastly, don’t let routine or boredom derail good habits. Variety is the spice of life! Try new snacks and meals. Switch up your meal schedule. Just be sure to keep those key components of protein, fiber, healthy fats, and complex carbs in mind. </span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>

Start small and then build on those habits over time. We’re here cheering you on! </span></p>
