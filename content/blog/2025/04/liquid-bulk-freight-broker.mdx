<h2><span style="font-weight: 400;">What is Liquid Bulk Shipping?</span></h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Liquid bulk shipping involves transporting bulk quantities of liquids &#8211; whether they be food-grade, chemicals, hazardous materials, or otherwise &#8211; in specialized tanker trucks. Proper handling, safety measures, and compliance are essential for a successful bulk liquid shipment.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Bulk liquid applies to a diverse range of cargo: </span></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Chemicals (examples: hydrogen peroxide, solvents, resins, mining chemicals)</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Water</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Milk</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Gasoline</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Alcohol</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Juice</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Syrup</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Vegetable oils</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Essential oils</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Vinegar</span></li>
</ul>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Each type of liquid has its own specifics to consider when being shipped, such as preventing contamination, preserving quality, and maintaining temperature. </span><span style="font-weight: 400;"></p>
<p></span></p>
<h2><span style="font-weight: 400;">What’s Involved with Managing Liquid Bulk Transport?</span></h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">The role of a Liquid Bulk Freight Agent is to oversee the transportation of the freight from pickup to delivery, ensuring safe and compliant shipments.</span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h3><span style="font-weight: 400;">Coordinate Carriers</span></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">One of the reasons Shippers rely on Freight Agents is because they have access to a larger carrier network than they would likely have on their own. Freight Agents must be sure to find vetted carriers with the proper certifications and equipment to transport each unique liquid bulk load. </span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h3><span style="font-weight: 400;">Utilize Technology </span></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">All Freight Agents must be able to track their loads in real time. This is where Transportation Management Systems (TMS) come into play. TMS makes it easier to provide real-time load updates to Shippers and provide solutions to problems that arise, or even before they do. This can save time for Shippers and provide peace of mind. </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;"><strong>A skilled Freight Agent can track capacity, anticipate demand, and arrange bulk liquid carriers with minimal downtime.</strong></span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h3><span style="font-weight: 400;">Ensure Regulatory Compliance and Safety</span></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Agents source carriers with specialized tanks, pumps, or connectors for easy loading and unloading of the specific bulk freight. This requires bulk liquid expertise, whether the freight is food-grade, hazardous material, or chemicals. Understanding the ins and outs of compliance is critical. To learn more, take a look at the </span><strong><a href="https://csa.fmcsa.dot.gov/safetyplanner/MyFiles/Sections.aspx?ch=25&amp;sec=81">DOT regulations for tanker shipments.</a> </strong></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h2><span style="font-weight: 400;">What to Consider When Specializing in Liquid Bulk</span></h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Liquid bulk is a profitable niche, but does take specialized skills, and has its own set of risks. </span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h3><span style="font-weight: 400;">Earning Potential</span></h3>
<p><b>Freight Agents typically earn a margin based on load rates.</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> Commissions vary by Broker, shipment complexity, and the company’s pricing model. Experienced Agents specializing in liquid freight can often command higher fees due to added complexity. </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">At Kopf, we pay 70/30 commission splits, which are some of the highest in the industry. We do not cap earnings as some brokerages do, so be mindful of that when choosing one to work with.</span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h3><span style="font-weight: 400;">Specialized Skills</span></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">In addition to excellent customer service, sales and negotiating skills, excellent communication, liquid bulk Freight Agents and Brokers </span><b>must also have a clear understanding of how to ship liquid freight </b><span style="font-weight: 400;">(we recommend this free downloadable “Liquid Bulk Shipping 101” from </span><a href="https://www.bulkconnection.com/liquid-bulk-shipping-101"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Bulk Connection</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">). </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">They must also be skilled at utilizing a vast carrier network of vetted carriers who have the proper driver certifications and equipment to transport the specific liquid freight. This does require specialized knowledge in understanding the legal compliance of transporting food-grade liquid or hazardous cargo. </span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h3><span style="font-weight: 400;">Risks</span></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Let’s not sugar-coat things. Bulk liquid freight can be hazardous and accidents with tankers can be catastrophic. Since liquid bulk is stored in one large tanker, rather than smaller containers, it is easier for an entire load to be damaged. Small leaks can become big problems. </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">In addition, tankers are a specialty type of vehicle. This results in two things: </span><b>longer lead times and higher rates. </b><span style="font-weight: 400;">It can be harder to find tankers since there are less available. It also costs carriers more to purchase, insure, and maintain tankers, which increases the cost to utilize them in transportation. For instance, tankers must be cleaned after every load and loads cannot be combined. So if a load is LTL (less than truckload) it is transported as is, without a rate reduction.</span></p>
<p><b>There are also different requirements for drivers of tankers.</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> They must obtain a specialized tanker endorsement. In addition, if they are transporting hazardous materials, drivers will also need hazmat certification. If freight is to be consumed by humans, Freight Agents must ensure the carrier is abiding by FDA and FSMA regulations.</span></p>
