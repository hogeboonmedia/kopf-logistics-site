<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Freight transportation is thriving. Not only is it continuing to grow as the demands for efficient movement of freight grows, it is also an essential part of the </span><a href="https://www.bts.gov/product/freight-facts-and-figures"><b>American economy.</b></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> And one of the most exciting and rapidly growing jobs within the industry is the role of Independent Freight Agent.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">With perks like low overhead, freedom to be your own boss, unlimited earning potential, and flexibility to work from anywhere with internet and phone access, becoming an Independent Freight Agent is a lucrative profession for the motivated entrepreneur. </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">At Kopf we’re often asked how to get started so we put together this guide to explain how to become an Independent Freight Agent step-by-step.  </span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h2><b>What Is an Independent Freight Agent? </b></h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">First things first, before looking at the steps to get started, we must be clear on the role of an Independent Freight Agent. </span></p>
<p><b>Independent Freight Agents are independent contractors who work as intermediaries between Shippers (who have the freight to be shipped) and Carriers (who do the actual transporting of freight) to arrange logistics services. </b></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">As business owners, Independent Freight Agents have their own customers and work on commission from their own office under the authority of a licensed Freight Broker. </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">This is why they are called </span><i><span style="font-weight: 400;">Independent</span></i><span style="font-weight: 400;"> Freight Agents. In contrast, a Freight Agent is an employee of a Freight Broker </span><b>(</b><a href="https://kopflogisticsgroup.com/2019/10/agent-vs-broker/"><b>read more about the differences here</b></a><b>).</b></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h2><b>What Does an Independent Freight Agent Actually Do?</b></h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">In the simplest of terms, the two main responsibilities of an Independent Freight Agent are sales and logistics management. </span></p>
<h3><span style="font-weight: 400;">Lead Generation and Sales</span></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">First, Agents need customers who have freight that needs to be shipped. They must be skilled at generating and securing leads (Shippers) through a variety of methods such as </span><a href="https://kopflogisticsgroup.com/2020/06/best-cold-call-script-for-freight-agents/"><b>cold calling</b></a><b>, </b><a href="https://kopflogisticsgroup.com/2026/01/a-guide-to-freight-broker-email-marketing/"><b>email marketing</b></a><b>, </b><a href="https://kopflogisticsgroup.com/2025/01/freight-broker-sales-pitch/"><b>mastering the sales pitch</b></a><b>,</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> and </span><a href="https://kopflogisticsgroup.com/2020/05/how-to-negotiate-freight-rates-top-tips-for-success-in-logistics/"><b>negotiation</b></a><b>. </b></p>
<h3><span style="font-weight: 400;">Logistics Management and Customer Service</span></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">The second main part of the role is managing logistics on behalf of clients. Technology called Transportation Management System, or TMS, helps Agents monitor loads from pickup to delivery. Agents must be skilled in communication and building relationships with Shippers and Carriers to consistently provide excellent customer service. </span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h2><b>Startup Costs to Become an Independent Freight Agent</b></h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Since Independent Freight Agents work under the operating authority of a Freight Broker, they are not responsible for the surety bond ($75,000 + $300 application fee), licensing fees, insurance, compliance systems, etc. </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">In addition to unlimited earning potential and the flexibility of being your own boss, an additional advantage to this career is it does not require considerable capital to get started. </span></p>
<p><b>Typical startup expenses:</b></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Computer and office equipment</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Phone/communication tools</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Internet</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Basic marketing tools like email and a customer management system</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Training (optional, but recommended)</span><span style="font-weight: 400;">
<p></span></li>
</ul>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Depending on the specifics you choose, you can realistically expect to spend $500 to $3,000. </span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h2><b>Income Potential for Independent Freight Agents</b></h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">This is where things get exciting!</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Independent Freight Agents are independent contractors, not employees. They are paid by commission from the brokerage in what is called a commission split (</span><a href="https://kopflogisticsgroup.com/2025/12/freight-broker-commission-split/"><b>which we explain in more detail here</b></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">). When booking a load, an Agent negotiates a rate with the Shipper and a rate with the Carrier. The difference between those two rates is the profit. That profit is then split between the brokerage and the Agent. This is commonly 50/50, 60/40, or 70/30.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">So, if you can build your book of business and maintain a strong track record of delivering results, </span><b>the long-term income potential is unlimited! </b></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">And, if you specialize in a certain niche, like</span> <a href="https://kopflogisticsgroup.com/2025/04/liquid-bulk-freight-broker/"><b>liquid bulk</b></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> or </span><a href="https://kopflogisticsgroup.com/2025/03/refrigerated-freight-broker/"><b>refrigerated freight</b></a><b>, </b><span style="font-weight: 400;">your earning potential increases even more. </span></p>
<p><b>Bottom line: You choose your success. </b></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h2><b>Step-by-Step: How to Become an Independent Freight Agent </b></h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">So, let’s get to the nitty-gritty of how to get started. </span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h3><span style="font-weight: 400;">Step 1: Decide if the Independent Agent Model Fits You</span></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">The role of an Agent is not easy but it is financially rewarding. To be successful, you must have an entrepreneurial mindset, excellent time management skills, be comfortable with commission-based income, and have excellent relationship building skills. </span></p>
<p><b>Ask yourself these questions: </b></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Do I want to work in the logistics industry?</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Do I want to manage my own business?</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Am I self-motivated?</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Do I enjoy sales? (Or am I willing to learn?)</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Am I a good communicator?</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Do I get energized by relationship building and networking?</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Do I prefer commission-based income? </span></li>
</ul>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">If you answered yes, then you’re ready to move to step 2!</span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h3><span style="font-weight: 400;">Step 2: Understand the Industry</span></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Being comfortable with the Agent model is the first step, but now you must grow in your logistics knowledge. Independent Freight Agents must understand how logistics works, types of freight, the different markets, lanes, load boards, and so forth. They must also understand how the sales process works.  </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">There are two ways to gain this knowledge: from previous jobs and through training classes. Relevant job experience includes former logistics professionals, such as </span><a href="https://kopflogisticsgroup.com/2024/03/truck-dispatcher-to-freight-agent/"><b>truck dispatchers</b></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">, and former sales professionals, since sales is a key part of success in this career.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">If you do not have relevant experience in both logistics and sales, we highly recommend </span><a href="https://kopflogisticsgroup.com/2024/10/freight-broker-training-classes/"><b>these training classes</b></a> <span style="font-weight: 400;">and</span> <a href="https://kopflogisticsgroup.com/2025/02/books-for-freight-brokers/"><b>books</b></a> <span style="font-weight: 400;">to kickstart your career. There is not required certification to become an Independent Freight Agent, but understanding the industry and being confident in sales is an absolute must. </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Now you’re ready for step 3. </span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h3><span style="font-weight: 400;">Step 3: Set Up Your Business</span></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Once you’ve gained industry knowledge, it’s time to establish your business. Choosing your business structure is the first step in starting your freight agency.  There are several options and best determined by consulting with an attorney or accountant to determine which best suits your needs.   </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">You’ll also need basic office essentials such as a phone, computer, internet, and any other communication tools helpful in conducting business.  A freight brokerage should supply you with their TMS which automates the freight tendering process.  </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Onto the next step.</span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h3><span style="font-weight: 400;">Step 4: Find a Licensed Freight Brokerage</span></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Since a brokerage carries the operating authority, you as the Independent Freight Agent must operate under the brokerage’s authority. A Freight Broker is in compliance with laws and regulations freeing the Agent to do the actual work of finding customers and managing loads. </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">It is important to find a brokerage that has strong financial backing, business office support when you need it, a commission split that supports your bottom line, and short turnaround on payouts. </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">As you search for brokerages, most often you’ll see a book of business is required. If you are a beginner and do not have customers, we highly suggest you consider taking a </span><a href="https://kopflogisticsgroup.com/2024/10/freight-broker-training-classes/"><b>training course.</b> </a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> Even with a small book of business, if you are motivated and willing to work, the right brokerage will consider giving you a chance. Don’t give up!</span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h3><span style="font-weight: 400;">Step 5: Get Customers </span></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Now that your business is set up and you’ve chosen a brokerage to contract with, it’s time to</span><a href="https://kopflogisticsgroup.com/2025/05/how-to-build-a-larger-freight-broker-book-of-business/"><b> build your book of business</b></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">, or customers for which you move freight. This is where having strong sales skills and mindset comes into play. </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">It isn’t easy, but it is a muscle you can build over time. Prospect, do cold outreach, leverage industry relationships, ask for referrals, network, and so forth. It must be part of your daily schedule. But also keep in mind, you must deliver on promises for the customers you do have. </span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h3><span style="font-weight: 400;">Step 6: Grow your Business </span></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">After you have customers and become more skilled at the job, you’re on your way to a financially rewarding career. As you gain experience, you’ll </span><a href="https://kopflogisticsgroup.com/2023/10/diversify-book-of-business/"><b>diversify your book of business</b></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> so you aren’t relying on one or two customers, seek accountability and support, and learn how to </span><a href="https://kopflogisticsgroup.com/2024/02/common-challenges-for-freight-agents/"><b>overcome common challenges</b></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">.</span></p>
