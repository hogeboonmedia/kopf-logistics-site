<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">In the freight industry, competition is high and attention is limited. That’s why staying visible to Shippers is just as important as offering competitive rates. Freight broker email marketing continues to be one of the most reliable ways to reach decision-makers, nurture relationships, and generate consistent Shipper leads </span><i><span style="font-weight: 400;">when it’s done right. </span></i></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">In part one of our three-part email marketing series, we’re breaking down how email fits into a Broker’s overall </span><a href="https://kopflogisticsgroup.com/2024/12/freight-broker-sales-tips/"><b>sales strategy,</b></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> what separates high-performing campaigns from ignored inbox clutter, and how to use email to build trust before a Shipper ever picks up the phone. </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Whether you’re just getting started with email or refining an existing strategy, this guide lays the groundwork for creating email campaigns that support steady business growth.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Let’s start at the beginning. </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">What actually is email marketing and why does it matter?</span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h2><span style="font-weight: 400;">What is Freight Broker Email Marketing and Why it Matters</span></h2>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Email marketing is simply using email to connect with customers. Freight Brokers can use it to communicate </span><a href="https://kopflogisticsgroup.com/2024/09/freight-brokers-add-value-to-shippers/"><b>value</b></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> to Shippers on their own schedule and stay top of mind over time. </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">According to 2025 research by Litmus, </span><a href="https://www.litmus.com/blog/infographic-the-roi-of-email-marketing"><b>most companies receive $10-$36 in return for every dollar invested</b></a> <span style="font-weight: 400;">in email marketing. This average return on investment is impressive and it makes email the most efficient direct marketing channel. </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Unlike social media, for instance, sending an email gets directly in front of the customer without relying on an algorithm. A typical social media post is only relevant for a few hours at most while an email remains accessible until the recipient decides to open or delete it. </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">And unlike cold calling, which may be an interruption to a Shipper, an email has the potential to connect with the Shipper on their schedule. That being said, at Kopf we recommend email  marketing as one part of an effective sales strategy. </span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>

<h2><span style="font-weight: 400;">How Email Marketing Fits Into a Freight Agent Sales Strategy</span></h2>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">As a Freight Agent you are continually working to fill your sales pipeline and</span> <a href="https://kopflogisticsgroup.com/2025/05/how-to-build-a-larger-freight-broker-book-of-business/"><b>grow your book of business.</b></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> Smart Agents know they cannot put all their “eggs in one basket” so to speak. Relationships turn sour. Staffing changes. Budgets change. The unexpected happens. </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">This is exactly why we recommend a </span><b>multi-pronged approach to your sales strategy </b><span style="font-weight: 400;">that includes: </span></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Building relationships and encouraging referrals</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Maintaining a professional and engaging online presence</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Making cold and warm calls</span> <a href="https://kopflogisticsgroup.com/2020/06/best-cold-call-script-for-freight-agents/"><b>(try this cold call script)</b></a></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">In person networking</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">And yes…email marketing</span></li>
</ul>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Email marketing builds upon the work you are already doing in the other areas of your sales strategy. It provides a way to stay connected, nurture leads, and so forth so that initial interaction doesn’t end. Simply ask existing and new leads to opt in to your email list and make it worth their while by offering value. </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">You can also use email marketing to reach out to cold leads. And, as an added benefit, when you use an email service provider, you can easily track data to help you make informed decisions about what kinds of emails work so you can rinse and repeat. </span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h2><span style="font-weight: 400;">Types of Email Campaigns for Freight Agents</span></h2>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">There are multiple types of emails Freight Agents can send. In the marketing world, each email you send is called a “campaign.” Here are three common types of campaigns.</span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h3><span style="font-weight: 400;">Cold Emails</span></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">When you send a cold or warm email to a lead, focus on how you solve specific problems they have. This could include problem lanes, unique challenges of their specific product that you have experience with (LTL, oversized, liquid bulk, hazardous materials, etc.). You can even </span><a href="https://kopflogisticsgroup.com/2023/12/cold-email-template/"><b>use our email template</b></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> to help get started. </span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h3><span style="font-weight: 400;">Nurture Emails</span></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Keep top of mind to new leads and current clients by sending nurturing emails that build your authority, trust, and likeability. This could be 1:1 emails you send directly to customers or mass emails you send as a newsletter. You might share longer case studies of the value you provided for a client and the results, testimonials, insights into industry trends, success stories, and so on. </span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h3><span style="font-weight: 400;">Transactional Emails</span></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">These are the day-to-day operational emails you send to welcome new clients, confirm deliveries, or thank clients for their trust. There is additional potential in these emails to include the option to subscribe to your newsletter, offer an incentive to give a referral or a testimonial, connect with you on a specific social platform, or so on. Transactional emails are often opened, so they provide valuable real estate to connect and build your authority. Take advantage of that!</span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h2><span style="font-weight: 400;">What Makes an Effective Email Campaign for Freight Agents</span></h2>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Whether you are sending a cold email, nurture email, transactional email, or another kind of email, there are always three important elements to keep in mind: </span></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Research (so you can personalize)</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Focus on solutions (so you provide value)</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Be concise (because Shippers are busy)</span></li>
</ul>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">When you research, focus on solutions, and are concise, your email campaign is more likely to be effective. </span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h3><span style="font-weight: 400;">Personalized</span></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">We cannot stress this enough: research, research, research! Do your research ahead of time to find as much information as you can before sending an email. This includes researching the name of the person you should contact which is generally the shipping, logistics, or warehouse manager. Use their name or the company’s name, the products they ship, the lanes they use, and so forth to personalize your email. This is especially important with cold emails.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">If you have a newsletter Shippers can subscribe to, include a “Name” field in your opt-in form. Then, when sending a mass email, you can add the code specific to your email service provider’s platform and your Shipper’s name will automatically appear in your email.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">As you become more familiar with email marketing, you can tag your customers based on characteristics such as lanes, products, and so forth. When you do this, you’re able to send highly specific content to segments of your list. </span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h3><span style="font-weight: 400;">Valuable</span></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Along with personalizing your emails, address specific needs and offer solutions in every email you send. This helps with customer retention and driving conversions. </span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h3><span style="font-weight: 400;">Actionable</span></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">End with a clear next step such as a brief call or meeting to discuss. Be sure to include an easy way to schedule with you, whether that be a scheduler (like Calendly), or simply list a few times that might work. </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Other specific calls to action could be: </span></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Book a call</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Reply back</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Quote my load now</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Get your free freight audit</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Find capacity for your __ lane (personalize the location)</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Get rates for __ to ___ (personalize the locations)</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Save 10% on your first shipment</span></li>
</ul>

<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Set aside 15 minutes at the same time each week to take a look at how your email campaigns performed. At a minimum, be sure to monitor the open rate, click rate (or conversion rate), and unsubscribes. </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">An easy way to do this is to make a simple spreadsheet with the metrics you want to track across the top. Then add your weekly data for every email you send. It could look something like this: </span></p>

<h2><span style="font-weight: 400;">Email Marketing Best Practices</span></h2>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">While we fully support email marketing at Kopf, we have seen Agents make mistakes that have resulted in negative ramifications, such as an email account being disabled or emails being sent to spam and never opened.  Therefore, the following tips are Kopf’s guidelines for email sending.   </span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h3><span style="font-weight: 400;">Tip #1 &#8211; Use an Email Service Provider</span></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">If you are sending bulk emails, it is necessary to send them through an external email service provider (ESP). </span><span style="font-weight: 400;">An ESP isn&#8217;t an inbox where you receive emails. It is an email sending tool.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">A few popular ESPs include: Constant Contact, Mail Chimp, and MailerLite. Basic plans usually start around $20 a month, with some offering free plans depending on how many tools you need and the size of your email list. It is easy to do a quick online search for the differences between pricing and features between platforms. </span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h3><span style="font-weight: 400;">Tip #2 &#8211; Use a Branded Email Address</span></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">While you can use yourname@gmail.com, we don’t recommend it. Instead, it builds credibility, trust, and is less spammy if your email has your agency info connected with it: yourname@youragency.com. Keep in mind some ESPs require you to have a branded email in order to use their services.</span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h3><span style="font-weight: 400;">Tip #3 &#8211; Use an Opt-In Form and Make it Easy to Unsubscribe</span></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">While you do not need a website, you will need a way to collect leads so you can send emails. If you do not have a website, ESPs usually provide landing pages where you can create a page that has an opt-in form embedded into it and these subscribers automatically get added to your subscriber list. Asking someone to opt-in means they want to receive your emails and if they choose to unsubscribe later, they may do so. This makes your email less likely to be seen as spam and in compliance with current regulations. </span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h3><span style="font-weight: 400;">Tip #4 &#8211; Be Brief and Make Your Emails Scannable</span></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Most people don’t read every word of an email. So, be sure to make yours easy to read and easy to scan. This includes using bold text for main ideas or your call to action, bullet points, and writing in short paragraphs or single sentences. </span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h2><span style="font-weight: 400;">Common Email Marketing Mistakes Freight Agents Make</span></h2>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">If you’re going to take the time to include email marketing as part of your sales strategy, take the time to do it with intentionality. This doesn’t mean hours crafting the perfect words, but rather make it a recurring event you block off in your weekly schedule. This could be 30-60 minutes. </span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h3><span style="font-weight: 400;">Unclear Goals</span></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Before you write any email ask yourself: </span><b>What is the action I want someone to take after reading this email? </b><span style="font-weight: 400;">Every email you write should have a clear call to action. </span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h3><span style="font-weight: 400;">Generic</span></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Don’t send generic blasts.</span> <span style="font-weight: 400;">This is likely to get your email in the spam filter. Instead of sending one-size-fits all, personalize your emails for different clients.</span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h3><span style="font-weight: 400;">Poor Subject Lines</span></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Even if you write the most amazing email, if it isn’t opened, it doesn’t matter. Sometimes Agents write subject lines that are vague or spammy. Instead, spark curiosity, be problem-focused, create urgency, be direct, be personalized. </span></p>
<p><b>Tip! </b><span style="font-weight: 400;">Write your content first and then go back and write your subject line. </span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h3><span style="font-weight: 400;">Not Tracking</span></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Work smarter, not harder! Keep track of which emails get opened and clicked. Use those emails or frameworks again instead of reinventing the wheel each time. </span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h2><span style="font-weight: 400;">Using Email Marketing to Build Long-Term Freight Relationships</span></h2>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Email marketing is worth the effort if you want to build long-term relationships with Shippers. For cold leads it can be a highly effective prospecting tool. For warm leads and existing customers, it is a powerful way to build your credibility and stay top of mind. Make your emails personalized, provide value by focusing on solutions, concise, and provide a clear call to action. </span></p>

<h2><span style="font-weight: 400;">Questions People Also Ask</span></h2>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>Does email marketing work for Independent Freight Agents?</b></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Yes. Email marketing works well when used consistently and paired with personalization. It helps Agents stay visible with Shippers and supports long-term relationship building.</span></p>
<p><b>How do Independent Freight Agents find emails for Shippers?</b></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Freight Agents typically source emails through industry directories, LinkedIn research, CRM databases, referrals, and outbound prospecting tools.</span></p>
<p><b>How often should Independent Freight Agents send marketing emails?</b></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Most Agents see the best results with consistent but non-intrusive outreach, such as weekly or biweekly emails, depending on the campaign goal.</span></p>
<p><b>What should a Independent Freight Agent include in a marketing email?</b></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Effective emails include a clear value proposition, a brief introduction, relevant industry context, and a simple call to action without being overly sales-focused.</span></p>
<p><b>Is cold email legal for Independent Freight Agents?</b></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Yes, cold email is legal when Agents follow </span><a href="https://www.ftc.gov/business-guidance/resources/can-spam-act-compliance-guide-business"><span style="font-weight: 400;">CAN-SPAM guidelines</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">, include proper identification, and provide a clear opt-out option. This is why we recommend using an email service provider. </span></p>
