<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Let’s face it! You’re a Freight Broker, NOT a copywriter. If you are sending emails as part of your marketing strategy (which we highly recommend and wrote about in</span><a href="https://kopflogisticsgroup.com/2026/01/a-guide-to-freight-broker-email-marketing/"><span style="font-weight: 400;"> part 1</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> and </span><a href="https://kopflogisticsgroup.com/2026/02/how-to-create-email-subject-lines-that-freight-shippers-will-open/"><span style="font-weight: 400;">part 2</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> of our email marketing guide) and you’re hearing crickets, let’s change that.  </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">The good news is, there are copywriting secrets out there to help you write high-converting emails that </span><i><span style="font-weight: 400;">actually</span></i><span style="font-weight: 400;"> get responses from Shippers.  So, if you are looking to improve your email marketing effectiveness, keep reading.</span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h2><span style="font-weight: 400;">Why Email Copy Matters for Freight Brokers </span></h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Freight is an extremely competitive market. This means your entire marketing strategy matters, from cold calling to networking to emails. If you send an email and it is opened by a Shipper (yay, your subject line worked!), now you have just seconds to keep their attention. What they see in the next moment either compels them to take positive action toward you or to pass you over and move on.</span></p>
<p><b>High-converting email copy directly impacts your response rates, trust, and ultimately your bottom line. </b></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Generic, unclear, or overly salesy emails aren’t going to make the cut. In fact, those emails might end up in spam. Personalized, short, to the point, value-driven emails with clear calls to action are the name of the game. But before we dig into the specifics of what works, it is important to start by thinking about </span><i><span style="font-weight: 400;">who </span></i><span style="font-weight: 400;">you are writing to. </span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;"><div class='et-box et-shadow'>
					<div class='et-box-content'></span><i><span style="font-weight: 400;">Note: In the remainder of this article, we’ll be using the term “Freight Agent” instead of “Freight Broker.” Although it is common to interchange Agent and Broker, these two roles are actually not the same (</span></i><a href="https://kopflogisticsgroup.com/2019/10/agent-vs-broker/"><i><span style="font-weight: 400;">these are the key differences</span></i></a><i><span style="font-weight: 400;">).</span></i><span style="font-weight: 400;"></div></div></span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h2><span style="font-weight: 400;">Understand Your Shipper Audience </span></h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Shipping managers are busy. That’s essentially the number one thing to keep in mind. </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Because they are busy, they need quick information. They are likely scanning emails rather than reading every word. And, they are not impressed by flashy copy, big words, or buzzwords they’ve heard a thousand times before. In fact, they’ve probably had dozens of cold emails land in their inbox along with yours. </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">So if you want to be top of mind, you have to stand out by giving them what they actually want: personalized, short, and to the point.</span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h3><span style="font-weight: 400;">Recommended Email Length for Shippers</span></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Keeping in mind that Shippers are busy, cold outreach emails typically only get viewed for about 11 seconds (</span><a href="https://www.lavender.ai/blog/cold-email-101"><span style="font-weight: 400;">more details here</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">). So, keep your emails </span><b>under 75 words</b><span style="font-weight: 400;">. For your initial emails, you should aim to be closer to only 25-50 words. Yes, that is very short! But keep in mind, you aren’t going to write everything about yourself or your services in one email. View your cold emails as a sequence that builds over a few weeks.</span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h3><span style="font-weight: 400;">The Best Times to Email Shippers</span></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">In general, Tuesday, Wednesday, or Thursday tend to perform best and 10 AM and 2 PM are peak times. Avoid Mondays and late Fridays. Also consider the Shipper’s time zone as it might be different from your own.</span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h2><span style="font-weight: 400;">How to Write Your Email Copy </span></h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">So how do you write an email that a Shipper will actually engage with? We recommend trying the</span><b> SCRAP formula. </b></p>
<p><img loading="lazy" decoding="async" class="aligncenter wp-image-20122 size-full" src="/blog-images/2026/02/scrap-email-formula.jpg" alt="5 part scrap email formula for freight agents " width="940" height="788" srcset="https://kopflogisticsgroup.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/scrap-email-formula.jpg 940w, https://kopflogisticsgroup.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/scrap-email-formula-480x402.jpg 480w" sizes="(min-width: 0px) and (max-width: 480px) 480px, (min-width: 481px) 940px, 100vw" /></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h3><span style="font-weight: 400;">S: Subject Line Grabs Attention</span></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">After you’ve finished writing your email, take time to craft a strategic subject line. A few ideas: use specific load details, include authentic urgency, or mention cost savings. Be sure to avoid spam trigger words like “URGENT” or words in all caps. Here are </span><strong><a href="https://kopflogisticsgroup.com/2026/02/how-to-create-email-subject-lines-that-freight-shippers-will-open/">subject lines freight Shippers will open.</a></strong></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h3><span style="font-weight: 400;">C: Clear Value Proposition</span></h3>
<p><b>Avoid starting your email with a generic statement like: “Hope this finds you well.”</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">This phrase is often used in generic sales emails, and it is easy for a Shipper to disregard an email simply by seeing a phrase like this right off the bat. </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Instead, make sure your email is a pattern interrupt. Get straight to the point and be a little more direct. Focus on opening lines that work: </span></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">reference recent shipping activity</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">mention mutual connections</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">lead with immediate value</span></li>
</ul>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">You can also lead with capacity solutions, emphasize reliability and communication, highlight competitive rates, or showcase success stories. </span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h3><span style="font-weight: 400;">R: Relevant to Shipping Needs</span></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Personalize your emails beyond the Shipper’s first name. Research their shipping lanes, mention recent industry news affecting their routes, reference seasonal shipping patterns and then be specific to the service you provide to meet those needs. </span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h3><span style="font-weight: 400;">A: Action-Oriented Close</span></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">End your email with a call to action (CTA). Optimize your CTA with specific action verbs such as call, reply back, or book. You can create urgency without pressure by providing specific times to connect with you. Whatever action you choose, include the clear next step. This will encourage the Shipper to engage with you. </span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h3><span style="font-weight: 400;">P: Professional Tone </span></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Remember you are writing to an actual human being who is busy, so be professional. Professional does not mean using buzzwords that are helpful to you but not necessarily to the Shipper (example: supply chain optimization, end-to-end visibility). </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">It also does not mean being overly aggressive (example: per my last email, please advise, I’m hoping to finalize, correct me if I’m wrong, etc.). </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Instead, it means being clear, to the point, and actually showcasing the value you provide. Clarity is kind.  And, being professional doesn’t have to be boring. It’s okay to use a GIF or meme to provide a bit of comedic relief from time to time. </span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h2><span style="font-weight: 400;">Email Templates for Different Scenarios </span></h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">The SCRAP framework works for every email you write. Here are a few samples for common emails you might write using this method. Customize these templates instead of using them word for word. They are </span><i><span style="font-weight: 400;">examples</span></i><span style="font-weight: 400;"> to get you started. </span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h3><span style="font-weight: 400;">Cold Email</span></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">This template works because it is short (under 75 words), personalized (lane specific), not overly aggressive, and provides a clear CTA. </span></p>
<blockquote><p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Subject: Quick Capacity Support for Your [Lane]</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Hi [Name],</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">I work with shippers moving freight in and out of [City/Region], helping secure reliable capacity even during tight markets. We specialize in [equipment type] with strong carrier coverage on [specific lanes].</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Would you be open to a 10-minute call this week to see if we can support any upcoming loads?</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">[Insert link to book a call]</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Best,</span><span style="font-weight: 400;"><br />
</span><span style="font-weight: 400;">[Your Name, Company]</span></p></blockquote>
<h3></h3>
<h3><span style="font-weight: 400;">Warm Referral Email</span></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">This email works because it is short (under 75 words) and leverages your connection as social proof. Referrals like these are gold!</span></p>
<blockquote><p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Subject: Referred to Support Your Shipping Needs</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Hi [Name],</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">[Referral Name] mentioned you manage freight for [Company]. We specialize in dependable [equipment type] capacity across [region] and focus on proactive communication and on-time delivery.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Would you be available for a brief call to explore where we can add value?</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">[Insert link to book a call]</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Best,</span><span style="font-weight: 400;"><br />
</span><span style="font-weight: 400;">[Your Name, Company]</span></p></blockquote>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h3><span style="font-weight: 400;">Value-Driven Email</span></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">When writing value emails, consider the Shipper’s potential pain points. This email works because it is short (under 75 words), clear, to the point, and offers a CTA.</span></p>
<blockquote><p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Subject: Reducing Delays on Your [Specific Lane]</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Hi [Name],</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Many shippers on the [Origin–Destination] lane are experiencing service inconsistency and rate changes. We’ve helped stabilize both by using vetted carriers and real-time tracking.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">If improving on-time performance or adding backup capacity is a priority, can we schedule 15 minutes this week?</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Reply back with which time works: [List a few time options]</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Warmly,</span><span style="font-weight: 400;"><br />
</span><span style="font-weight: 400;">[Your Name, Company]</span></p></blockquote>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h2><span style="font-weight: 400;">Measuring Your Email Success</span></h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Now that you’ve considered your audience, used the SCRAP formula and written your email, it’s time to track the results. If you are sending emails and not paying attention to what is working, you are missing out on valuable information so you can work smarter going forward. At a minimum, you should be monitoring the open rate, reply, and unsubscribes. You can track this information on a spreadsheet. </span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h3><span style="font-weight: 400;">Open Rate</span></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">If an email is opened, it tells you the subject line is effective. Anything above 30% is strong. Keep note of the subject lines that perform well (and those that don’t) so you can use these as models in the future. </span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h3><span style="font-weight: 400;">Reply Rate</span></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">When a Shipper takes the time to reply back to your email, you know you’re onto something good. If your replies are low, your email might be too generic, weak call to action, or an unclear value proposition. Anything above 8% is strong. </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Keep in mind, most often most Shippers don’t reply until after multiple emails or points of contact. Email #3 is usually key. When tracking replies, it is helpful to take note which touch point the email was as well. </span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h3><span style="font-weight: 400;">Unsubscribes</span></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">If a Shipper unsubscribes or opts out, it means they are not connecting with your copy. Anything above 1% is concerning. The messaging in these emails must be changed.</span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h2><span style="font-weight: 400;">Common Email Mistakes Freight Brokers Make</span></h2>
<p>Even if you understand your audience, follow the SCRAP formula, and track key metrics, it is still easy to make these five common mistakes.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h3><span style="font-weight: 400;">Failing to Follow-Up</span></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">One email is not typically going to close a sale. That is why it is important to develop a f</span><b>ollow-up email sequence </b><span style="font-weight: 400;">of 3-5 emails over 2 weeks. So instead of putting everything you want to say in one email, break it up into a sequence. Be sure to vary your emails by including value, social proof, and authentic urgency. </span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h3><span style="font-weight: 400;">Not Being in Compliance</span></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">In 2023, the Federal Trade Commission set regulations for all commercial email. If you are sending any email promoting your business, you must be in compliance of this law. If not, you are subject to substantial penalties. So, it is essential that all Agents follow the </span><a href="https://www.ftc.gov/business-guidance/resources/can-spam-act-compliance-guide-business"><span style="font-weight: 400;">CAN-SPAM requirements</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">. A few of the main requirements include: </span></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Don’t use misleading from/reply-to information</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Don’t use deceptive subject lines</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Include the option to unsubscribe or opt-out </span></li>
</ul>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">It really isn’t hard to be in compliance, and if you are using an email service provider like MailChimp, Constant Contact, etc., they make this process even easier. </span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h3><span style="font-weight: 400;">Poor Deliverability</span></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">If you are sending 50+ emails a day, email filters might start to flag your emails as spam. So instead of being sent to the Shipper’s email inbox, they are instead sent straight to spam. And once in spam, your emails typically stay there. This is not good! </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">So instead of focusing on getting X number of emails sent, focus on the quality of the emails you are sending by implementing the other tips in this article. </span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h3><span style="font-weight: 400;">Not Optimizing for Mobile</span></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">When you are typing on a computer, it is easy to forget the Shipper may be reading your email on their mobile device. You can optimize for mobile view by keeping subject lines short (under 50 characters), use short paragraphs, include clickable phone numbers, and use bullet points so it is easy to scan the content. </span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h3><span style="font-weight: 400;">Generic Subject Lines</span></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Even if you write the best email, if the subject line isn’t intentional, your email might be overlooked. Be relevant, personalized, concise, or solution-focused (</span><a href="https://kopflogisticsgroup.com/2026/02/how-to-create-email-subject-lines-that-freight-shippers-will-open/"><span style="font-weight: 400;">see these tips). </span></a></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h2><span style="font-weight: 400;">Use High-Converting Email Copy to Grow Your Book of Business</span></h2>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Lead generation isn’t optional as a Freight Agent. So, as you are looking to <a href="https://kopflogisticsgroup.com/2025/05/how-to-build-a-larger-freight-broker-book-of-business/">grow your book of business</a> and work your sales pipeline, email can be an effective part of your strategy if it is used intentionally.
