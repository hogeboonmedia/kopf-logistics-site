<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Lead generation isn’t fun, but it must be part of a Freight Agent’s daily routine. If you want to </span><strong><a href="https://kopflogisticsgroup.com/2023/10/diversify-book-of-business/">diversify your book of business</a></strong><span style="font-weight: 400;">, sending a cold email can be one way to start the conversation with prospects. Keep reading to discover our best cold email tips and an unconventional &#8211; but effective &#8211; template you probably haven’t seen before. </span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h2><span style="font-weight: 400;">What is a Cold Email?</span></h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">A cold email is your initial contact with a potential customer. Often agents try to close the sale in their first cold email, but that is not wise. Think of it as a way to capture a shipper’s attention, introduce yourself, and offer them a solution to a problem they may have.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Shippers are busy. Some are more likely to read an email at their convenience rather than answer a cold call at an inconvenient time. However, most shippers actually prefer the person-to-person contact of a cold call. So, it is best to include both in your strategy. </span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h2><span style="font-weight: 400;">How to Get Your Email Opened</span></h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Now that the decision has been made to write a cold email, you might not think your subject line is very important. But, your subject line is critical!  It doesn’t matter how much time you spend writing your email if it isn’t opened. </span></p>
<p>A good subject line needs to be compelling, relevant, or provide a clear benefit so the prospect will open it. <span style="font-weight: 400;">Keep the following best practices in mind. </span></p>
<ul>
<li><b>Keep it short.</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> The best subject lines are 40-50 characters or about 7-9 words. </span></li>
<li><b>Personalize it. </b><span style="font-weight: 400;">Try using the shipper’s name, commodity or specific needs. </span><i><span style="font-weight: 400;">Example: Chris, Guaranteed Bulk Transport Available from Atlanta to Chicago.</span></i></li>
<li><b>Use numbers. </b><span style="font-weight: 400;">Provide numerical details to make the headline more intriguing. </span><i><span style="font-weight: 400;">Example: [Client] saved 25% on shipments in just 2 months! </span></i></li>
<li><b>Use emotional words. </b><span style="font-weight: 400;">Emotion drives action. Search “emotional words for email subject lines” and you’ll find a list of ideas such as: helpful, value, satisfy, quality, guaranteed, etc.</span></li>
<li><b>Make it urgent. </b><span style="font-weight: 400;">Use words like now, today, right now, etc. </span></li>
<li><b>Ask a question. </b><span style="font-weight: 400;">Build curiosity by framing your subject as a question. </span><i><span style="font-weight: 400;">Example: Stressful problem lanes? Let me tackle them right now! </span></i></li>
<li><b>Use how. </b><span style="font-weight: 400;">Turn your subject into a “how to.” </span><i><span style="font-weight: 400;">Example: How to save money on your next shipment. </span></i></li>
</ul>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">While you shouldn’t use every strategy in one headline, experiment to find what is most effective for you. </span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h2><span style="font-weight: 400;">How to Write an Email That Stands Out</span></h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">After you’ve captured the shipper’s attention and they’ve opened your email, your content needs to stand out. How? Use these tips.</span></p>
<h3><b>Personalize it. </b></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">As with the</span><strong><a href="https://kopflogisticsgroup.com/2020/06/best-cold-call-script-for-freight-agents/"> best cold call script</a></strong><span style="font-weight: 400;"><strong>,</strong> you must do your research. Take a look at their LinkedIn profile and website. Know their first name, what commodity they ship, and potential problems they might have that you could solve. You can even reference something they recently said on social media.</span></p>
<h3><b>Keep it conversational. </b></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Write like you are having a conversation with your prospect. Don’t worry about being formal. Speak in first person as if you are actually talking to them. If you are being human, you will stand out.</span></p>
<h3><b>Focus on one main idea. </b></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Email marketers focus on the rule of one. Write as if you are speaking to one person about one idea with one call to action. Yes, there is a lot you want to say, but make sure you focus on one big idea. This should be the value you provide for them. This is what makes you unique. </span></p>
<h3><b>Keep it short. </b></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Your prospect is going to want to see exactly what the email is about without having to read every word. They do not want to see huge paragraphs or a long email. Otherwise, they’ll likely close out because they might not have the time to keep reading. So, use short sentences and short paragraphs. </span></p>
<h3><b>Make it easy to read. </b></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Don’t write a corporate style email. Yes, be grammatically correct, but you don’t need to be concerned about traditional paragraph structure. In fact, a paragraph can even be one sentence long. Other ways to make it easy to read is using bold text, bullet points, or numbered lists. </span></p>
<h3><b>End with a clear call to action. </b></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">What do you want them to do after reading your email? You could let them know you’ll be calling later this week, request they add you to their load list email, or ask them to reply back with “tell me more.” </span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h2><span style="font-weight: 400;">Sample Cold Email Template</span></h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">If you are looking for a template you can use to just copy and paste, please reconsider. We guarantee a formal, generic, impersonal email is not effective. Put in the work! One of our agents recently shared a response he received after sending a standard email using a professional template. </span></p>
<blockquote><p><i><span style="font-weight: 400;">“I get 100 of these a day. It’s just a canned speech about your brokerage. I could care less about the brokerage. You’re my agent. What are you going to do for me? Why should I choose you over the other 150 that called me this week? Just be real. Be honest.” </span></i></p></blockquote>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">The agent responded back authentically.</span></p>
<blockquote><p><i><span style="font-weight: 400;">&#8220;Hey look, help a brother out. I have experience and work for a great company. I’d love to have the opportunity to show you what I can do. I’m getting back in the industry, I have two kids and I need the business. I will work my tail off for you. I’m trying to get going and I need someone to give me a break.&#8221;</span></i></p></blockquote>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Surprisingly, it worked. This is because p</span><span style="font-weight: 400;">eople want to work with <em>real</em> people. Make sure to show how you are different and the value you will provide. Try a template like this. </span></p>
<blockquote><p><i><span style="font-weight: 400;">Hello [prospect name]! I am [your name] and I would love to move your freight. Let me tell you why I’m different.</span></i></p></blockquote>
<blockquote><p><i>[Insert a numbered or bulleted list with specifics of how you’ll be different or ways you can provide value such as: I will answer the phone 24/7, I will treat your freight as if I owned it, etc.]</i></p></blockquote>
<blockquote><p><i>I know your product is not sitting in a warehouse unmoved, and I know you get lots of calls from agents wanting to move it.  But I also know you probably have difficult lanes you could use help with.  Let me work those lanes for you. Let me do it once to show you what I can do. I’ll take your difficult lanes and prove myself.</i></p></blockquote>
<blockquote><p><i>[Insert call to action such as: I’ll follow up with a phone call later this week.] </i></p>
<p><i>[Sign off]</i></p></blockquote>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Use this template to get started, but make it your own.</span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h2><span style="font-weight: 400;">What to Do After Sending Your Email</span></h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Most Freight Agents fail to follow up </span><a href="https://kopflogisticsgroup.com/2018/03/5-helpful-tips-for-independent-freight-agents-before-and-after-the-cold-call/"><b>after making cold calls</b></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> and sending cold emails. Create a system so you have an automated alert to send follow up emails to shippers who do not respond. It is helpful to have at least two follow up email templates drafted so you can easily copy, paste, and personalize your note. </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Another good idea is to reach out by phone. Email and phone work well together. Sometimes shippers may block email addresses if they think your email is spam, so if you send a follow up email and still do not hear back, make sure to call. </span></p>
<p>Lastly, be prepared for rejection. <a href="https://kopflogisticsgroup.com/2020/02/freight-agent-motivation/"><strong>Stay motivated</strong></a> and keep going!</p>
