<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">One of the </span><a href="https://kopflogisticsgroup.com/2023/06/freight-agent-benefits/"><span style="font-weight: 400;">benefits of being a Freight Agent</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> is setting your own schedule. At Kopf, we recognize each agent is different. So, we’re sharing three helpful ways to structure your day for Freight Agent productivity. Keep in mind there isn’t a “one-size-fits-all” technique. It takes experimentation to find what works for you. </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Use this guide to stop feeling overwhelmed, find your unique scheduling method, and increase your efficiency. </span></p>
<h2><span style="font-weight: 400;">Method #1 &#8211; Time Blocking</span></h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">This popular scheduling method takes preparation. Essentially, you are telling yourself how to spend your time before the day begins. You define blocks (or hours) of your day for specific tasks. Your blocks could be as simple as morning, afternoon, and evening. Or, they could be as detailed as every hour. Use a digital calendar or paper planner to create your schedule. </span></p>
<h3>Sample Schedules</h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Here is how a basic time-blocked schedule could look: </span></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Early Morning: Load status updates</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Morning: Find trucks and fill loads</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Mid-day: Lunch and self-care</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Early afternoon: Check on morning and afternoon loads </span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Late afternoon: Prospecting</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Evening: Tomorrow’s schedule and self-care</span></li>
</ul>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">An hourly time-blocked schedule could look like this:</span></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">5-6: Self-care </span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">6-7: Current load status updates </span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">7-11: Find trucks and fill loads </span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">11-12: Lunch </span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">12-1: Check on morning loads</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">1-2: Check on afternoon loads</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">2-3: Prospecting and cold calls</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">3-4: Pickup kids from school</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">4-5: Verify all loads complete</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">5-6: Tomorrow’s schedule</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">6-8: Family time</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">8-9: Spouse and self-care </span></li>
</ul>
<h3>Keys to Success</h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">If you choose to start a time-blocked schedule, there are three keys to success. </span></p>
<ol>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><b>Schedule the most important tasks first. </b><span style="font-weight: 400;">Current customers take priority over prospecting. </span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><b>Don’t overlook self-care. </b><span style="font-weight: 400;">Schedule time for the things that fill your cup and strengthen your significant relationships. It&#8217;ll help your <a href="https://kopflogisticsgroup.com/2021/01/freight-agents-balance-work-home-life/">work-life balance.</a></span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><b>Leave extra space when possible.</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> This makes it easier to adjust your schedule as needed for unexpected problems or tasks that take longer than planned. </span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><b>Plan time in your schedule to set your schedule.</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> You’ll want to include weekly, daily, and even mid-day planning time. For example, Friday at 5 you plan your schedule for the upcoming week, each day at 5 you review the next day’s schedule, and you check the rest of your day before logging off your computer for lunch. </span></li>
</ol>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Cal Newport, author of </span><i><span style="font-weight: 400;">Deep Work, </span></i><span style="font-weight: 400;">is an expert in productivity and time blocking is his top scheduling method. He writes, </span></p>
<blockquote><p><i><span style="font-weight: 400;">“A 40 hour time-blocked work week, I estimate, produces the same amount of output as a 60+ hour work week pursued without structure.”</span></i></p></blockquote>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">If this seems overwhelming to you, it may not be the best fit. But if you are intrigued, you might want to give time blocking a try.</span><a href="https://calnewport.com/deep-habits-the-importance-of-planning-every-minute-of-your-work-day/"><span style="font-weight: 400;"> </span></a></p>
<h2><span style="font-weight: 400;">Method #2 &#8211; Most Important Task</span></h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">The second scheduling method for Freight Agent productivity is called the most important task method. This method does not involve a massive to-do list. Instead, it forces you to step back and decide what tasks are most critical for your day. </span></p>
<p><strong>Here’s how it works. </strong></p>
<ol>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Ask yourself, “What must be done today?” </span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Focus on these 1-3 tasks.</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Don’t do anything else until you’ve accomplished your most important tasks.</span></li>
</ol>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">You may already be unknowingly implementing part of this strategy. As a Freight Agent, the most important tasks for the day most likely already happen in the morning due to the nature of freight. </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">To take it a step further, intentionally choose to focus on these tasks so you can frame your day as successful. As a result, you will likely have more energy and motivation for the rest of your day knowing you did what was most important first. Don’t overlook the power of this positive mindset. </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">James Clear, the author of </span><i><span style="font-weight: 400;">Atomic Habits, </span></i><span style="font-weight: 400;">is a productivity expert and supporter of this method. He writes,  </span></p>
<blockquote><p><i><span style="font-weight: 400;">“If you do the most important thing first each day, then you’ll always get something important done.”</span></i></p></blockquote>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">If this method sounds interesting, you might want to try a task management app such as </span><a href="https://todoist.com/"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Todoist</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> or </span><a href="https://www.omnigroup.com/omnifocus"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Omnifocus</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> to keep you on track. </span></p>
<h2><span style="font-weight: 400;">Method #3 &#8211; Focus Sessions</span></h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">The third scheduling method that increases productivity involves focus sessions. Decide what task you’re going to work on, then set a timer and focus on it. After the timer stops, take a break, then repeat. There are two main variations: the Pomodoro Method and the 90-minute focus technique.</span></p>
<p><b>Here’s how the Pomodoro Method works.</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> </span></p>
<ol>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Choose a task.</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Set a timer for 25 minutes and work on the task.</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Take a 5-minute break.</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Repeat.</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">After 4 sessions, take a longer 15-30 minute break.</span></li>
</ol>
<p><b>This is how the 90-minute focus technique works. </b></p>
<ol>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Work 90 minutes.</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Rest 20-30 minutes.</span></li>
</ol>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Both of these methods emphasize the importance of focus and rest. They aim to work with your body’s energy rather than against it. </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Keep in mind you don’t have to implement these sessions all day long. For instance, you might have a work sprint time where you intentionally set a timer to help keep you focused as part of your day. Remember, do what works for you. </span></p>
<h2><span style="font-weight: 400;">Your Freight Agent Productivity Method</span></h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">As a Freight Agent, you hit the ground running from that first sip of coffee. Before your day begins, you already have a massive to-do list floating around in your head. Freight doesn’t take a vacation. But, you aren’t designed to work around the clock constantly feeling in reactive mode. </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Ease the overwhelm by setting your schedule. </span><span style="font-weight: 400;">Try time blocking, the most important task method, focus sessions, or a combination of these to find what works for you. Be sure to prioritize time for self-care and what matters most to you and you’ll be on your way to becoming </span><span style="font-weight: 400;">a </span><a href="https://kopflogisticsgroup.com/2023/02/high-performers/"><span style="font-weight: 400;">high-performing Freight Agent.</span></a></p>
