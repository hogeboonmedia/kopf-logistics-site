<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">As a Freight Agent, you already know </span><a href="https://kopflogisticsgroup.com/2023/10/diversify-book-of-business/"><span style="font-weight: 400;">diversifying your book of business</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> is critical to a successful agency. But are you targeting the best industries? If you are looking to grow your profits, keep reading.</span></p>
<h2><span style="font-weight: 400;">Types of Freight </span></h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">First, it is important to consider the types of freight you can move as a Freight Agent: road, rail, ocean, and air. This article focuses on road freight moved by truck. Even within this type of freight, there are different types of trucks used depending on the goods that need to be transported.</span></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><b>Truckload (FTL): </b><span style="font-weight: 400;">a full truckload of freight moved from starting point to destination without loading in between</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><b>Temperature-Controlled:</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> used for shipping produce that must be kept from freezing, or food products that must be frozen or refrigerated (</span><a href="https://kopflogisticsgroup.com/2022/02/how-long-do-reefer-trailers-last/"><span style="font-weight: 400;">reefer</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">) </span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><b>Open-Deck: </b><span style="font-weight: 400;">Special freight that does not fit inside a traditional enclosed trailer</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><b>Less-Than-Truckload (LTL):</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> a partial truckload of freight not large enough to take up the capacity of an entire truck </span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><b>Bulk Transport:</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> large quantities of dry or liquid goods shipped without packaging</span></li>
</ul>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">As an Agent, you should take time to think about the pros and cons of each type of freight. For example, LTL shipments have less wasted space and are more economical so it may be attractive to shippers wanting to save money but also leads to increased freight transit times.  For a temperature-controlled truck, a mechanical failure could result in a lost load and lost profits. While not common, bulk transport can be risky for goods such as flammable liquids. However, some of these risks also leave room for a greater need to move these types of freight. </span></p>
<h2><span style="font-weight: 400;">The Most Profitable Industries</span></h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Second, Freight Agents should also dig into the most profitable industries. Have you heard the phrase “riches in the niches”? It’s true! Many</span><a href="https://kopflogisticsgroup.com/2023/02/high-performers/"><span style="font-weight: 400;"> successful Agents </span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">become specialists and move specific types of goods or goods for specific industries. It is best to start out with one niche and then expand over time. </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">There are hundreds of industries you can target. However, some are more profitable and in-demand than others. We recommend pursuing the following 8 industries for the greatest profit potential.</span></p>
<ul>
<li><b>Construction Equipment: </b><span style="font-weight: 400;">manufacturers of heavy equipment </span></li>
<li aria-level="1"><b>Chemicals: </b><span style="font-weight: 400;">hazardous and non-hazardous</span></li>
<li aria-level="1"><b>Building Supplies</b><span style="font-weight: 400;">: this includes lumber, steel, and any manufacturer producing building supplies such as siding, shingles, and so forth</span></li>
<li aria-level="1"><b>Oil and Gas: </b><span style="font-weight: 400;">there are many large and ancillary drilling companies as well as related suppliers that produce the equipment they need </span></li>
<li aria-level="1"><b>Medical:</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> this includes medical supplies, devices, and pharmaceuticals</span></li>
<li aria-level="1"><b>Automobile:</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> manufacturers and suppliers of new and old vehicles, auto parts, and accessories</span></li>
<li aria-level="1"><b>Food and Beverage: </b><span style="font-weight: 400;">everything from produce to bottled water, this is an essential and huge industry</span></li>
<li aria-level="1"><b>Consumer Goods and E-Commerce: </b><span style="font-weight: 400;">this is another large industry and includes almost anything in your house from shampoo to furniture</span></li>
<li style="list-style-type: none;"></li>
</ul>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">If there is an industry you want to learn more about, simply Google it. Take note of the various types of goods within the industry and make a list of companies selling these products. You might start your search with larger well-known companies, but dig deeper to find smaller companies within each niche as well. Use your list as a springboard for </span><a href="https://kopflogisticsgroup.com/2020/03/generate-better-leads/"><span style="font-weight: 400;">lead generation. </span></a></p>
