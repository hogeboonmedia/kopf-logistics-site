<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Are you looking for remote Freight Broker jobs? Do you also want to learn more about what it means to be a Broker? If so, you are in the right spot. In this blog post, we’ll answer the following questions: </span></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">What is a Freight Broker?</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Should you become a Freight Broker?</span></li>

</ul>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Let’s start by defining a Freight Broker. </span></p>
<h2><span style="font-weight: 400;">What is a Freight Broker?</span></h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">A Freight Broker acts as a middleman to arrange the transport of goods by matching available trucks with shipper loads/needs. </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">They are also responsible for the financial aspects of their brokerage company. To become a Freight Broker you will first need experience.  Then you will need capital for the following:</span></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Training expenses</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Licensing and registration</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Surety bond expenses</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Business insurance</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Software </span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Marketing</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Other business expenses</span></li>
</ul>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">If this seems overwhelming, you may want to consider becoming a Freight Agent instead. </span></p>
<h2><span style="font-weight: 400;">Should You Become a Freight Broker?</span></h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Not necessarily. Often when someone calls expressing an interest in being a Freight Broker, it becomes clear they actually want to </span><a href="https://kopflogisticsgroup.com/2022/06/become-a-freight-agency-owner/"><span style="font-weight: 400;">become a freight agency owner</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">. Freight Agents are not required to have the financial stability a Broker needs to conduct business. </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">There are key </span><a href="https://kopflogisticsgroup.com/2019/10/agent-vs-broker/"><span style="font-weight: 400;">differences between a Broker and an Agent</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">. Essentially a Freight Agent works as an independent contractor from their home or office under the authority of a licensed Freight Broker. Agents find shippers, negotiate fees, and plan pickups and deliveries. They connect loads to trucks and trucks to loads. The brokerage provides financial stability and resources for the Agent. </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">If you have experience moving freight, a book of business, and plenty of capital to start your own brokerage, you may want to be a Broker. However, if you seek low start-up costs, financial backing, and a support team, being an Agent might be a better fit. </span></p>
<h2><span style="font-weight: 400;">How Do Freight Brokers and Freight Agents Make Money?</span></h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">To make money, you’ll need sales skills to </span><a href="https://kopflogisticsgroup.com/2022/10/5-top-ways-for-independent-freight-agents-to-land-a-customer/"><span style="font-weight: 400;">land customers</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">. Frankly, it takes hard work, perseverance, and consistency. One of the sales methods both Freight Brokers and Freight Agents must master is cold calling. Start with just five calls a day. Then keep increasing the number of calls you make as you gain confidence. If you’re not sure what to say, </span><a href="https://kopflogisticsgroup.com/2020/06/best-cold-call-script-for-freight-agents/"><span style="font-weight: 400;">try this cold call script</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Brokers earn money directly from moving freight and from their Agents’ sales. An Agent earns a sales commission on each load they move. The amount of this commission varies between brokerages.  </span></p>

<h2><span style="font-weight: 400;">Where Can You Find Freight Broker Jobs?</span></h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Now that you understand what a Freight Broker is, if you really want to become one, one question remains. Where can you find remote Freight Broker jobs? If you are a Freight Broker, you will likely start your own company or brokerage. This means you’ll be working for yourself and won’t need to look for a job. </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">However, if you want to become a Freight Agent, you’ll seek to work as an independent contractor by looking for a Freight Broker or brokerage to work with. So the question becomes, how do you find a good brokerage?</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Be on the lookout for a company that is well established and has been in business for many years. Also, pay attention to their commission rate, if they have commission caps, how they support their Agents, and the tools they provide. </span></p>
