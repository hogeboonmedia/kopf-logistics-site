<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">The American trucking industry is growing. In fact, <strong>over 60% of all freight</strong> in the US is moved by trucks. One of the most rapidly growing jobs within the industry is the role of Independent Freight Agent. But, what does an Independent Freight Agent do? If you’re curious to learn the job description and hear from someone who is successfully doing it, keep reading.</span></p>
<h2><span style="font-weight: 400;">What It&#8217;s Like</span></h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Kevin, one of Kopf’s talented Independent Freight Agents, describes his role this way.</span></p>
<blockquote><p><span style="font-weight: 400;">“Number one, I secure customers. I make solicitation phone calls and send emails to secure accounts that need help shipping their truckloads of product across the country.  </span><span style="font-weight: 400;">Once I secure the customer, my role is basically to provide pricing on these shipments and then find carriers to haul the shipments. Then I facilitate the appointments to make sure everything runs smoothly. Once these shipments are delivered, I profit from the difference.”</span></p></blockquote>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">To put it another way, Independent Freight Agents are business owners, have their own customers, and work on commission from their home or office under the authority of a licensed Freight Broker to coordinate freight shipments. As their name implies, they are independent contractors.  </span></p>
<h2><span style="font-weight: 400;">Job Description</span></h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Independent Freight Agents work as intermediaries between shippers and carriers to arrange logistics services. They negotiate rates, book shipments, and manage logistics on behalf of their clients. </span><strong><a href="https://kopflogisticsgroup.com/2020/06/best-cold-call-script-for-freight-agents/">Cold calling</a></strong><span style="font-weight: 400;"><strong>,</strong> networking, and lead generation are an essential part of an Agent’s typical day. </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Often, the terms </span><strong><a href="https://kopflogisticsgroup.com/2019/10/agent-vs-broker/">Freight Agent and Freight Broker</a></strong><span style="font-weight: 400;"> are used interchangeably, but they are actually different roles. Unlike a Freight Broker, a Freight Agent doesn’t have their own operating authority and legally can’t arrange the movement of freight on their own. This takes the legal and financial burden off an Agent and makes it easier to get started in this career. </span></p>
