<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">In the world of transportation logistics, Freight Brokers play an important role. When a company produces a product they need to ship, the logistics of getting that load successfully from point A to point B involves a few key players in the supply chain, one of which is a Freight Broker. But what exactly is a Freight Broker, what do they do, how do they make money, and are they even worth it? Let’s take a closer look.</span></p>
<h2><span style="font-weight: 400;">The Role of a Freight Broker in the Supply Chain</span></h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">A Freight Broker acts as a middleman or intermediary to arrange the transport of goods by matching available trucks with shipper loads. This can range from a one-person operation to a larger multinational freight brokerage company with many employees. In order to operate in the United States, Brokers are legally required to be licensed by the Federal Motor Carrier Safety Administration</span><a href="https://www.fmcsa.dot.gov/"><span style="font-weight: 400;"> (FMCSA)</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> and secure a surety bond.</span></p>
<p><strong>Oftentimes the terms Freight Broker and Freight Agent </strong><span style="font-weight: 400;"><strong>are used interchangeably in the logistics industry, but they are not the same.</strong> </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Agents work on commission from their home or office under the authority of a licensed Freight Broker to coordinate freight shipments. The Freight Broker, then, is responsible for the financial aspects such as invoicing shippers, paying carriers, extending credit, paying Freight Agents, and assisting with claims.</span></p>
<h2><span style="font-weight: 400;">What Exactly Does a Freight Broker Do?</span></h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">A Freight Broker’s job is to oversee the financial aspects involved with moving freight. If the brokerage is a larger company, this may be managed by their accounting department. These financial tasks include: </span></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Invoicing shippers for services rendered</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Ensuring shippers pay invoices promptly and following up as needed</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Paying carriers (trucking companies or truck drivers who actually move the freight)</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Paying Freight Agents working under their authority the commissions they earned</span></li>
</ul>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">In addition to overseeing the financial aspects of the business, a Freight Broker spends time each day marketing and recruiting new Freight Agents while also supporting their existing ones. </span></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Onboarding Agents </span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Researching, obtaining, and providing Agents with the tools they need to succeed</span></li>
</ul>

<h2><span style="font-weight: 400;">The Duties of a Freight Broker</span></h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Along with the financial and recruiting tasks mentioned above, at times a Freight Broker will also perform tasks generally managed by Agents operating under their FMCSA authority. The services provided are as follows: </span></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Generate new shipper leads. This includes preparing quotes and </span><a href="https://kopflogisticsgroup.com/2020/05/how-to-negotiate-freight-rates-top-tips-for-success-in-logistics/"><span style="font-weight: 400;">negotiating prices.</span></a></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Secure reliable carriers, this could be from a trucking company or owner operators, to haul loads and develop positive relationships with them.</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Ensure the shipping process is completed successfully by managing, tracking, resolving problems that arise in transit with carriers or truck drivers, and providing shippers with updates.</span></li>
</ul>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">A Freight Broker will also oversee the operations of their company, including staff development, marketing, customer relations, and standard operating tasks. </span></p>
<h2><span style="font-weight: 400;">How Freight Brokers Make Money</span></h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Since Freight Brokers are middlemen between shippers and carriers, they make their money from the margin between what they or their Freight Agent charges a shipper and how much they pay the carrier. Commissions vary from one transaction to the next. Freight Agents earn a commission off each load from the Broker under whose authority they are working. To earn more money, both Brokers and Agents need to move more freight and focus on </span><a href="https://kopflogisticsgroup.com/2023/01/increase-profit-margins/"><span style="font-weight: 400;">increasing profit margins.</span></a></p>
<h2><span style="font-weight: 400;">What is the Most a Freight Broker Can Make?</span></h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Just as any business has the potential to make an unlimited profit, the same is true with Brokers. The answer is unlimited. There isn’t a cap on how much a Broker can make. Keeping in mind the terms Freight Broker and Freight Agent are often used interchangeably, the most important factor in determining how much money can be made is the amount of effort you are willing to invest to develop and grow your business. Freight brokering is hard work whether you are a Broker or an Agent. At Kopf, our Agents enjoy a 70/30 commission split with weekly payment. </span></p>
<h2><span style="font-weight: 400;">How Does a Freight Broker Get Loads?</span></h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Lead generation is a key part of a successful freight brokerage. In theory, the more freight moved, the greater the profit. This means it is critical to find loads that need shipping. There are many </span><a href="https://kopflogisticsgroup.com/2020/03/generate-better-leads/"><span style="font-weight: 400;">ways to get loads</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">, but it takes a consistent ongoing effort, including: </span></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Reach out to existing customers</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Encourage referrals</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Cold call (</span><a href="https://kopflogisticsgroup.com/2020/06/best-cold-call-script-for-freight-agents/"><span style="font-weight: 400;">use these tips</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">)</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Cold email (</span><a href="https://kopflogisticsgroup.com/2023/12/cold-email-template/"><span style="font-weight: 400;">try this template)</span></a></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Research shippers at destinations or along routes of current loads</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Research shippers within the same industry of current shipments</span></li>
</ul>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">This is the same way a Freight Agent secures loads as well.</span></p>
<h2><span style="font-weight: 400;">Are Freight Brokers Worth It?</span></h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Yes! Freight Brokers are the right partner for both shippers and carriers. They help many shippers find the best price and often lower rates to keep costs low through their network of motor carriers. Also, they provide expertise and knowledge needed to ensure each shipment arrives safely at its destination. Good Freight Brokers are a resource or value, a needed resource, and one that is growing in demand. </span></p>
