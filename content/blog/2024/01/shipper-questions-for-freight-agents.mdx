<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Nearly everything consumers buy will have to pass through a Freight Agent at one point or another on its journey. The</span><a href="https://data.bts.gov/stories/s/Freight-Transportation-the-Economy/6ix2-c8dn"><span style="font-weight: 400;"> $1.5 trillion</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> shipping industry is essential for business and everyday life. As such, it is important shippers choose the right freight agency to ensure their goods are handled properly without unexpected issues. </span><span style="font-weight: 400;">If you already know </span><a href="https://kopflogisticsgroup.com/2022/01/shippers-how-to-choose-the-right-independent-freight-agents/"><span style="font-weight: 400;">why you need a Freight Agent</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> (not to be confused with a </span><a href="https://kopflogisticsgroup.com/2019/10/agent-vs-broker/"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Freight Broker</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">), how do you choose one? There are many factors to consider. Use these shipper questions to help you secure the best Freight Agent to facilitate the shipment of your cargo.</span></p>
<h2><span style="font-weight: 400;">#1 &#8211; Certification</span></h2>
<p><b><i>What certification and licenses do you hold?</i></b></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">The first shipper question is a legal one. Freight Agents should be required to have the proper documents to demonstrate they’re legally allowed to perform their services. You can check with your regional authorities or ask potential Agents for copies of their certification. It’s imperative to make certain they’re qualified and authorized to perform services in your area .</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Keep in mind that an Independent Freight Agent (independent contractor) and Freight Agent (employee) operate under the authority of a Freight Broker or brokerage. An Agent should be able to tell you about the licenses and FMSCA authority held by their brokerage.</span></p>
<h2><span style="font-weight: 400;">#2 &#8211; Experience</span></h2>
<p><b><i>How long have you been in business?</i></b></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Experience is important as it means they’ll likely understand the process and know the intricacies of the industry. They’ll understand the laws and regulations of shipping various types of cargo. Even better, given their length of time in the industry, they may have strong industry connections that allow a seamless shipping process, making things easier for you. </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Look for an Agent with at least a few years experience in the field. This will help make sure your goods won’t end up lost, damaged, undelivered, or affected by inexperience or lack of knowledge about the shipping process. </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">However, everyone needs someone to give them their first shot. If you have a good sense about a new Agent without much experience, ask about the experience they do have in transportation or related fields and why they want to get into freight. </span></p>
<h2><span style="font-weight: 400;">#3 &#8211; Specialization</span></h2>
<p><b><i>What methods of shipment and locations do you specialize in?</i></b></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Shipping is a very complicated business. There are many different types of services provided depending on the cargo size, weight, value, destination, country/region/state, and more. Your Agent should specialize in the kind of services you need to ship your goods. This means they’ll have experience with this particular type of service and know the ins and outs of it. </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">That being said, if the Agent does not specialize in your niche or location, listen for their response. Do they have a network they can reach out to for support? Are they driven to learn about it? Have they shipped a similar type of good? Sometimes their lack of experience in your particular product doesn’t mean they aren’t the best fit for you.</span></p>
<h2><span style="font-weight: 400;">#4 &#8211; References</span></h2>
<p><em><strong>Where can I view a list of references from previous customers? </strong></em></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Choose an Agent who is happy to provide references from past clients. Ask for contact details so you can speak to those references directly. These people will be able to tell you about their experiences with the Agent and whether or not they were satisfied with the service.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">References allow you to gain insight into their professionalism, competency, reliability, and trustworthiness. You should also look at the Agent’s online customer reviews where possible to get a better idea of their level of customer service. </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">In addition, you can ask the Agent to share about how they overcame a past shipping issue to see how they solve problems. </span></p>
<h2><span style="font-weight: 400;">#5 &#8211; Best Offer</span></h2>
<p><b><i>What is your best offer for shipping my goods?</i></b></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Shipping Agents have different</span><a href="https://kopflogisticsgroup.com/2021/12/what-influences-freight-shipping-costs/"><b> shipping costs</b></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> based on volume, weight, size, and country/region/state of origin. Ensure you ask for this information before signing contracts or agreements. Compare prices to understand what is available. You’ll get a complete picture of </span><span style="font-weight: 400;">what each agency has to offer and gain insight into competitive pricing to avoid being overcharged. </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">The price, of course, should depend on the level of services offered as well as the quality of services expected. Cheaper prices may not provide the quality of service you require. Be prepared to choose a reasonable price for the services you expect.</span></p>
<h2><span style="font-weight: 400;">#6 &#8211; Timeline</span></h2>
<p><b><i>How soon can you begin shipping my goods?</i></b></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Some services and destinations require more lead time than others. If the service you need cannot begin immediately, it may affect your schedule for moving or shipping your cargo. Ask your Agent how soon they can start shipping your goods to ensure they can meet any deadlines you have set.</span></p>
<h2><span style="font-weight: 400;">#7 &#8211; Pre-Shipping Documentation</span></h2>
<p><b><i>What documentation do I need to provide before shipping my goods?</i></b></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">There are many different documents both the shipper and the recipient need to facilitate a smooth delivery. Some documents may also need to come from a third party, such as an insurer. Make sure you know what document your Agent requires so you can provide them with it before they begin working on your shipment.</span></p>
<h2><span style="font-weight: 400;">#8 &#8211; Cargo Insurance</span></h2>
<p><b><i>What types of cargo insurance do you offer?</i></b></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Several companies provide cargo insurance for goods being shipped. Each company has its requirements about what types of services or goods they will insure. Ask your Agent to discuss providers and define requirements before shipping. This insurance should be held by the freight brokerage under whose authority a Freight Agent is operating. The Freight Agent should be aware of the broker’s insurance.</span></p>
<h2><span style="font-weight: 400;">#9 &#8211; After Delivery Expectations</span></h2>
<p><b><i>What should I expect after my shipment has arrived at its destination?</i></b></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Shipping does not end when your goods reach their final destination. Your Freight Agent should be able to provide you with information about what happens next. For instance, what communication can you expect to receive? If there is something an Agent has done or you in the past that was helpful or did not do what you wished they had, now is the time to ask.</span></p>
<h2><span style="font-weight: 400;">#10 &#8211; Complaints</span></h2>
<p><b><i>How do I go about filing a complaint if necessary?</i></b></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Like all businesses, shipping agencies sometimes make errors or don’t meet customer expectations. If this happens during an international shipment, different bodies handle disputes depending on how you shipped your goods. Make sure you know how to resolve complaints in the event something does not go as expected. Inquire about how they handle disputes so you will be prepared for anything that might happen.</span></p>
<h2><span style="font-weight: 400;">#11 &#8211; Difference</span></h2>
<p><b><i>What makes you different?</i></b></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">This is the last, but perhaps most important of all the shipper questions. Be sure to ask for any additional information that might be relevant and helpful. Top Freight Agents should not hesitate to share what makes them stand out. It might be how easy it is to reach them, their work ethic or willingness to get the job done, and so forth. This question will help you make an informed decision about whether or not this Agent can provide the kind of shipping services that are best for you and your cargo.</span></p>
<h2><b>Final Thoughts</b></h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Don’t attempt to go it alone as a shipper. When you’re ready to look for a Freight Agent, these 12 shipper questions will help you find the best fit for your unique needs. Use this list to ensure you find an Agent who can serve you well for the years to come. </span></p>
