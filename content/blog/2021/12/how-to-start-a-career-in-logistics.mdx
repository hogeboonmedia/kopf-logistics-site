<p class="p1">It&#8217;s estimated that <a href="https://www.oberlo.com/blog/online-shopping-statistics" rel="noreferrer"><span class="s1">2.14 billion people</span></a> now shop online. With trillions of products shipping all over the globe, there’s a growing need for competent logistics professionals.</p>
<p class="p1">If you&#8217;re looking for independence and an opportunity to own your business, consider a career in logistics. It doesn&#8217;t matter if you&#8217;re a new graduate or a seasoned worker who wants to change direction, there&#8217;s a great opportunity waiting for you!</p>
<p class="p1">So, what should you do to get started? Here are some helpful tips for launching your career!</p>
<p class="p3"><strong>Launching a Career in Logistics</strong></p>
<p class="p1">While a college education is not necessary for launching a logistics career, it can be extremely helpful. That said, it&#8217;s also possible to start in an entry-level position within an organization and work your way up the ranks to <a href="https://kopflogisticsgroup.com/2021/01/freight-agents-balance-work-home-life/" rel="noreferrer"><span class="s1">freight agent</span></a> before becoming an agency owner yourself.</p>
<p class="p1">It&#8217;s a great way to learn to meet deadlines, work in teams, and learn how to research data. These are all skills you&#8217;ll need when you&#8217;re in charge of moving goods and products, shipping in bulk, and storing commodities around the country and the world.</p>
<p class="p3"><strong>Working with an Employment Agency can be Helpful</strong></p>
<p class="p1">Without experience, one way to gain the skills to become a Freight Agent is to work with an employment or staffing agency. These companies specialize in placing people in entry-level positions in many different industries. Some positions are temporary, but they give workers a chance to experience different work environments and learn new skills.</p>
<p class="p1">This is a great way to get your foot in the door with a logistics company and begin learning the skills to someday open your own agency.</p>
<p class="p2"><span class="Apple-converted-space">    </span></p>
<p class="p3"><strong>Study a Second Language</strong></p>
<p class="p1">If you aren&#8217;t already bilingual, studying a second language can give you an <a href="https://www.unitedlanguagegroup.com/blog/benefits-bilingualism-business" rel="noreferrer"><span class="s1">edge in business</span></a> and in the logistics field. As your responsibilities increase, you&#8217;re more likely to have to work with international teams.</p>
<p class="p1">Having some fluency in another language will make you more attractive to shippers, allowing you to increase your business.</p>
<p class="p3"><strong>Get Comfortable Handling Data</strong></p>
<p class="p1">The logistics field generates a lot of data, from order records to scheduling, to warehousing. You should be comfortable handling large amounts of data and working with spreadsheets.</p>
<p class="p1">It&#8217;s possible you&#8217;ll work with a company with its own proprietary software program.<span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>Having database skills and computer proficiency will carry you far in your logistics career.</p>
<p class="p3"><strong>Research Logistics Companies</strong></p>
<p class="p1">For more career tips, research websites of top logistics companies.<span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>Researching a career in logistics will help you identify the responsibilities of a Freight Agent and provide you with a better understanding of the daily workflow.<span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span></p>
